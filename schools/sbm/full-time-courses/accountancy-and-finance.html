About the Course

This is for you if you aspire to be a tech-savvy accounting professional. You will be equipped with the critical skills to work in diverse industries.

As a qualified accountant, you will be the company’s driver of change, analysing financial data and helping the management visualise key insights into business trends.

 

This diploma lets you . . .

  • Hone your competencies in emerging areas that are in demand in the accounting industry, such as digital accounting, risk management, forensic investigation and sustainable finance.
  • Gain first-hand experience in working with key industry partners that include the Big Four public accounting and commercial firms through our six-month internship programme.
  • Accelerate your professional journey with accreditation and module exemptions at local and overseas universities and professional bodies such as the Association of Chartered Certified Accountants (ACCA), Chartered Institute of Management Accountants (CIMA) and Institute of Chartered Accountants in England and Wales (ICAEW).

Benefits

  • Get a head-start in your profession with transdisciplinary competencies and in-demand skill sets
  • Widen your horizons with overseas opportunities such as foreign exchange programmes, internships and immersive study trips.
  • Look forward to exciting careers with companies like the Big Four public accounting firms, banks, financial institutions and governmental agencies.

The NYP-PCM for the Diploma in Accountancy & Finance consists of:

22 Competency Units (CmUs)
Within each Competency Unit, the skills and knowledge of different disciplines are integrated and taught holistically. This enables learners to fulfil specific work tasks upon completion.

6 Course Competencies
Each Course Competency consists of a collection of Competency Units that develops specific learning outcomes and competencies.

3 Competency Canvases
Each Competency Canvas consists of a collection of Competency Units and a Work- Integration Unit (WIU), which is a workplace-contextualised project. Competency Canvases help learners develop and demonstrate their abilities to perform work tasks at a higher proficiency.

1 Internship Programme

11 General Studies Modules

These modules allow you to explore other areas of interest beyond your chosen area of specialisation.

Completing the required Competency Units will equip you with six Course Competencies:

1.       Assurance & Forensics Analytics

2.       Business Communication & Innovation

3.       Business Intelligence & Data Science

4.       Business Operations & Resource Management

5.       Financial Accounting & Automation

6.       Sustainable Finance, Treasury & Financial Management

You will also need to complete Work-Integration-Unit (WIU) which can be in the form of an internship and/or project that mirror work situations where students can demonstrate their skills and competencies.

Application of Business Fundamentals [60 hours]

In current workplace, employers are looking for graduates who have a holistic understanding of today’s complex and dynamic business environment and have the ability to connect the dots of business functions, relationships, systems and resources. Thus, having a good understanding of business fundamentals is essential for all business professionals. This CmU covers the ecosystem of business organisation and environment and provides the grounding and interlinks of the key business functions and skills of business - human resource management and administration, supply chain management, finance and accounting, marketing, international business, operations management, laws and policies.


Applied Economics for Business [60 hours]

In a dynamic business environment, individuals and business organisations need to apply microeconomic and macroeconomic analyses to evaluate the impacts of changing economic climate. In this CmU, learners will acquire the knowledge on how individuals and businesses make decisions about consumption and production based on market structures, and the role of government in influencing these decisions. Learners will also be equipped with analytical skills to assess the impact on businesses from changing economic factors such as a country’s national income, business cycle, price levels of goods and services, fiscal & monetary policies and international trade issues.


Applied Statistics for Business [60 hours]

In a fast-paced business environment, employers are looking for graduates who are able to analyse, understand and apply data to make optimal business decisions.  In this CmU, learners will be equipped with the knowledge and skills to transform raw data into useful information and apply the insights obtained to make informed business decisions. Specifically, learners will be taught descriptive statistics, regression and correlation analysis, discrete and continuous probability distributions, statistical estimation, hypothesis testing, and fundamental artificial intelligence (AI) concepts and tools to solve real-life business problems. 


Business Accounting & Analysis [60 hours]

With the increasing importance of the role in accounting in any business organisations, employers are looking for graduates who have good understanding of the business assets and able to perform financial accounting and analysis for corporations in the real world.


In this CmU, learners will be equipped with the knowledge and skills in accounting treatments to measure, record and report the various types of assets in the Statement of Financial Position. Learners will be able to account for companies, including constructing Statement of Cash Flows and Statement of Changes in Shareholders’ Equity. 


Business IT & Spreadsheet Modelling [60 hours]

The ability to analyse is an indispensable skill in the business world as it helps organisations make better data-driven decisions.  In this digital world, it is also equally important to be able to ensure effective data storing and reliable data flow by using the right technology. In this CmU, learners will gain essential knowledge and skills in analysing data using system software in presenting business solutions effectively. This CmU will also provide opportunities for learners to build and enhance their knowledge in key digital transformations. Specifically, learners will be equipped with knowledge in relevant data structures, databases and cloud computing.


Financial Accounting & Interpretation [60 hours]

Employers are always looking for graduates who have good understanding of accounting concepts and practices to properly account for business transactions and assess business financial performance. In this CmU, learners will learn to apply accounting concepts and principles for the recording of business transactions, and the preparation of year-end adjustments and financial statements. In addition, learners will acquire competencies in interpreting financial statements and information for various stakeholders. 


Professional Business Communication [60 hours]

Professional business communication that is driven in companies determine the success of their operations and business.  Strong communication skills are needed in AI-driven and sustainable workplaces.  In this CmU, learners will master must-have business communication skills to achieve professional success. This CmU builds learners' communication competencies in applying effective business writing strategies and persuasive presentation techniques to influence and win over co-workers and customers in person and online. Learners will also sharpen their communication proficiencies to enhance personal branding and workplace relationships that advance careers. Learners will also master the art of business storytelling to better connect with audiences, especially for sustainability.


Strategic Business Communication [45 hours]

Companies engage in strategic business communication.  Skilled strategic communications cultivate positive relationships, promote inclusive social well-being, and safeguard their organisations' reputations through effective interpersonal and digital communication.  A strategic focus includes communicating sustainability effectively tailored to different circumstances and cultural contexts.  In this CmU, learners will demonstrate defining strategic communication skills to thrive in increasingly diverse, multicultural and digital workplaces. Specifically, learners will apply strategic and critical thinking that are part of strategic communication skills to achieve bias-free communication, foster inclusiveness and negotiate challenging exchanges in person or virtual.


Sustainable Finance [60 hours]

In the fast-transforming financial services industry, employers are looking for graduates who have sound knowledge of financial markets, investment products and the drivers of risk and returns in a portfolio with considerations for environmental, social and governance factors while observing financial viability. Banks are further realising the moral imperative of the immense power they wield in funding and raising issues of sustainability. This resolve can be achieved by investing in research and development of alternative energy sources and in businesses that adopt equitable and sustainable practices. In this CmU, learners will be equipped with the knowledge towards evaluating how finance interacts with environmental, social, and governance (ESG) factors, analysis of risk and return profiles of asset classes, and the conduct of security analysis within the context of portfolio construction based on given investment objectives.


User-Centred Design [45 hours]

Companies need to thrive in a business environment often termed as VUCA - volatile, uncertain, complex and ambiguous - and to survive and thrive in this world of disruption, the name of the game is "innovation". Companies value employees who can innovate and develop solutions that meet the needs of its users. This CmU will provide opportunities for learners to practise and experience the key stages of Design Thinking, such as being empathetic to users, identifying pain-points, generating creative ideas, prototyping and testing the innovations, as learners apply the various tools and methods through collaborative learning.

Accounting System & Blockchain [60 hours]

In a digital environment, it is necessary for accountants to have strong conceptual and technical foundation in applying key business processes to accounting information systems and distributed ledger technology.


Learners will be equipped with knowledge and skills to setup and create business transactions related to business processes in a cloud accounting system.  Furthermore, learners will learn about distributed ledger technology, including blockchain and its technical setup for use in suitable business processes.


Audit & Assurance [60 hours]

In a continuously evolving complex operating business environment, it has resulted in a greater call for transparency in the assurance engagements. Organisations must be constantly prepared to combat a myriad of risks and must ensure high quality corporate results are delivered. In this CmU, learners will be equipped with the fundamental principles of assurance that will enable them to undertake audit work at specific stages of an assurance engagement.  Learners will develop competencies in executing internal audit engagements based on International Professional Practices Framework (IPPF).


Corporate Reporting [60 hours]

Accounting professionals will need to constantly keep abreast of changes in both financial and non-financial reporting standards and frameworks. In this CmU, learners will learn about the framework of financial reporting and gain an understanding of the statutory disclosure requirements in the preparation of financial statements. In addition, there will be opportunities for learners to construct financial statements at both company-level and group-consolidated level, including relevant disclosure requirements, in accordance with the Singapore Financial Reporting Standards (SFRS). With the growing emphasis on sustainability, it is important for learners to be competent to discuss on the Environmental, Social and Governance (ESG) factors presented in organisations' corporate reporting.


Data Science Fundamentals and Analytics [60 hours]

As businesses move toward a digitalised future, data will be gathered in ever greater quantity and variety. The skill to understand and make sense of data therefore becomes ever more important. In this CmU, learners will gain an awareness of the concepts of data science, with a specific focus on business analytics. Learners will be able to analyse data using exploratory data analytics through the application of data visualisation and will also be able to create a basic machine learning model for predictive analytics. These skills to organise and prepare data and apply data analysis techniques will allow learners to gain insights from data, aid business functions and support business decision making.


Digital Accounting & AI [60 hours]

Emerging technologies are key drivers impacting jobs and skills in the accounting sector. In this CmU, learners will learn various technologies and tools and be equipped with technical skills that are highly sought after by business organisations.


Digital Technology Adoption for Business (WIU) [45 hours]

Productivity and innovation are two key pillars put forth in the Accountancy Roadmap, essential to the growth of the accountancy sector in Singapore. Accountants will be required to adopt and harness innovative technologies to drive greater efficiency amidst the competitive business landscape. In this WIU, learners will adopt a collaborative learning process, including analysing the requirements for digital business transformation, assessing and deploying digital enablement tools to best respond to business needs. Learners will learn to apply the knowledge and skills in practical business settings in order to achieve desired business outcomes, such as increased productivity and quality decision making.


Sustainability in Business Project (WIU) [30 hours]

In a fast-paced business environment, employers are looking for graduates who can plan and implement sustainability strategies for companies effectively. In this WIU, learners will synthesise their knowledge and devise sustainability strategy for a business case or project. Specifically, learners will demonstrate competencies in areas such as sustainability readiness assessment, synthesising sustainability-related risks, opportunities, and market development, and conducting organisational sustainability report analysis. 


Risk Management and Forensic Investigation [75 hours]

With volatility in the business environment, climate change and financial crimes, the world business’ backdrop is challenging and calls for greater emphasis on risk management and forensic accounting activities.  Resilience in businesses is increasingly becoming pertinent for survival, thus elevating the need for sound risk management policies to be in place, including fraud risk management. Forensics in accounting is fast becoming an emerging domain area within the accounting industry, gaining its foothold especially with its leverage on the use of analytics for financial forensic investigations.


Taxation [60 hours]

Tax computation and planning is vital in a dynamic business environment to help maximise business profits and cashflow. In this CmU, learners will have opportunities to analyse how Singapore taxation applies to individuals and business entities and develop competencies of two tax collection mechanisms, Withholding Tax - a key consideration in international transactions, and Goods and Services Tax (GST) – Singapore’s tax on domestic consumption.


CmUs/WIUs in Year 3

  • Corporate Financial Management
  • Company Law & Practice
  • Cost & Management Accounting
  • Innovation & Entrepreneurship Project (WIU)
  • Innovation & Viable Business Development
  • International Finance & Treasury
  • Internship Programme (WIU)

Corporate Financial Management [45 hours]

Employers are always looking for graduates who have good understanding of the banking and finance industry, as well as having an in-depth understanding of short-term and long-term financing for organisation. In this CmU, learners will be equipped with the knowledge and skills to analyse and value bond and equity and apply the insights obtained to make informed capital structure and working capital decisions. Specifically, learners will develop competencies in applying time value of money concepts, discount cash flow, dividend policy and cost of capital to solve investment and fund-raising problems.


Company Law & Practice [45 hours]

In Singapore, a company is a commonly used entity that is established to engage in a business. With Singapore’s reputation as a regional and global hub for businesses and the increasingly complex environment that businesses now operate in, it is of paramount importance for those engaged in business to be familiar with the legislation and rules that apply to companies. Through this CmU, learners will gain knowledge about the full legal spectrum of the life cycle of a company, from its inception – the role of directors, members and officers of a company, the legal principles surrounding how companies are governed and financed, the schemes available for financially troubled companies – to the process of dissolving or shutting down a company.


Cost & Management Accounting [60 hours]

Employers are always looking for graduates who have the knowledge and analytical skills to support management in business operations. In this CmU, learners will be equipped to compute and analyse the cost of a product or service, including measures to control the key costs within a business operation. Learners will be able to prepare operational budgets in the budgeting and forecasting process, identify opportunities for performance measurements and improvement, analyse financial information to assist management in operational and ad-hoc decision-making.


Innovation & Entrepreneurship Project (WIU) [45 hours]

This WIU culminates the competencies learnt in Year 2 and 3. Learners are required to apply the knowledge and skills gathered previously to test the viability of a business in preparation to launch it.  Learners will adopt the Agile Scrum framework to develop the skills to validate business model using Design Thinking and Lean Business Model Canvas methodologies, execute prototype testing to determine Problem-Solution Fit and Minimum Viable Product (MVP) testing to determine Product-Market Fit. They will also develop entrepreneurial attributes such as perseverance, resilience and risk-taking. This WIU will also provide opportunities for coaching from experienced business innovators currently from the industry, funding opportunities for pre-incubation and adoption of the business plan by industry partners.


Innovation & Viable Business Development [45 hours]

Studies have shown that although products and service innovation are essential for the initial success of a business, business model innovation can deliver more lasting competitive advantage, particularly in this disruptive era. This CmU aims to develop learners’ capability in business model innovation by applying Lean Business Model Canvas (LBMC) methodology on a business case. By analysing the evolving business models in terms of the integrated components of LBMC, learners develop in-depth knowledge and skills in enhancing business competitive advantage and value creation.


International Finance & Treasury [45 hours]

For an international hub like Singapore, international finance plays an important role in facilitating international trade and cross border exchange of goods and services. It is imperative for banking professionals to develop in-depth knowledge and be equipped with the tools and techniques to forecast exchange rates, raise capital in international debt and equities markets, and ascertain the economic performance of foreign markets to gain a competitive edge in the highly globalised world economy. Through this CmU, learners will develop competencies in evaluating the roles, operations, financing methods and instruments employed in international finance and treasury departments, performing the functions and operations of corporate treasury department by developing appropriate strategies to manage the risks and exposures faced, and applying various treasury products and strategies for hedging according to strategic goals of multinational firms. Learners will also be competent in analysing the behaviour of interest rates and exchange rates movement in the international financial system with reference to corporate objectives such as profit maximisation, risk management, and critical evaluation of the various methods of payment used in international trade by outlining the payment and goods flow in the documentary collection and documentary credit processes.


Internship Programme (WIU) [960 hours]

Internship provides valuable work experiences and opportunities to develop learners' competencies in a real work environment. This internship programme comprises a 24- week attachment to a local or overseas business, public or governmental organisation. The attachment provides learners with real-life working experiences and develops in them the right work attitude, a practice-oriented and hands-on mindset, and the ability to work with diverse people in an organisation. The learners' performance during their attachment is graded. Participating organisations will provide a detailed training programme, which may include project assignments and/or rotational on-the-job training.
