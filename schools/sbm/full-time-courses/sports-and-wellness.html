About the Course

This is for you if you want to play an integral part in Singaporeâ€™s ambition to promote sport and wellness as a way of life. Be among Singaporeâ€™s next generation of sport business and wellness specialists. Lead our nationâ€™s charge towards preventive care. Learn to manage and operate businesses.

This unique course combines sport, wellness and business through hands-on experiences, applied training and real-world case studies.

Be equipped with professional competencies to build a valuable portfolio. Your training will include enhanced internships and industry exposure to better prepare you for the workplace.

This diploma lets you . . .

  • Fuel your passion for sport, active lifestyle and healthy living. Enable others to pursue lifelong wellness goals.
  • Engage with lecturers and gain experience during hands-on practical trainings and internships.
  • Learn to become an agile team player and passionate sport and wellness practitioner in Singapore and the region.

Benefits

  • Develop expertise in sport business, sport marketing and managing sport and hybrid events.
  • Cultivate a broad range of skills in sport coaching and deepen your knowledge in fitness training and exercise prescription.
  • Acquire capabilities in health coaching and develop skills in behaviour modification and wellness applications.

The NYP-PCM for the Diploma in Sport & Wellness Management consists of:

20 Competency Units (CmUs)
Within each Competency Unit, the skills and knowledge of different disciplines are integrated and taught holistically. This enables learners to fulfil specific work tasks upon completion.

6 Course Competencies
Each Course Competency consists of a collection of Competency Units that develops specific learning outcomes and competencies.

2 Competency Canvases

Each Competency Canvas consists of a collection of Competency Units and a Work- Integration Unit (WIU), which is a workplace-contextualised project. Competency Canvases help learners develop and demonstrate their abilities to perform work tasks at a higher proficiency.

1 Teaching Enterprise Project

1 Internship Programme

11 General Studies Modules

These modules allow you to explore other areas of interest beyond your chosen area of specialisation.

Application of Business Fundamentals [60 hours]

In current workplace, employers are looking for graduates who have a holistic understanding of todayâ€™s complex and dynamic business environment and have the ability to connect the dots of business functions, relationships, systems and resources. Thus, having a good understanding of business fundamentals is essential for all business professionals. This CmU covers the ecosystem of business organisation and environment and provides the grounding and interlinks of the key business functions and skills of business - human resource management and administration, supply chain management, finance and accounting, marketing, international business, operations management, laws and policies.


Applied Economics for Business [60 hours]

In a dynamic business environment, individuals and business organisations need to apply microeconomic and macroeconomic analyses to evaluate the impacts of changing economic climate. In this CmU, learners will acquire the knowledge on how individuals and businesses make decisions about consumption and production based on market structures, and the role of government in influencing these decisions. Learners will also be equipped with analytical skills to assess the impact on businesses from changing economic factors such as a countryâ€™s national income, business cycle, price levels of goods and services, fiscal & monetary policies and international trade issues.


Applied Statistics for Business [60 hours]

In a fast-paced business environment, employers are looking for graduates who are able to analyse, understand and apply data to make optimal business decisions.  In this CmU, learners will be equipped with the knowledge and skills to transform raw data into useful information and apply the insights obtained to make informed business decisions. Specifically, learners will be taught descriptive statistics, regression and correlation analysis, discrete and continuous probability distributions, statistical estimation, hypothesis testing, and fundamental artificial intelligence (AI) concepts and tools to solve real-life business problems.


Business IT & Spreadsheet Modelling [60 hours]

The ability to analyse is an indispensable skill in the business world as it helps organisations make better data-driven decisions.  In this digital world, it is also equally important to be able to ensure effective data storing and reliable data flow by using the right technology. In this CmU, learners will gain essential knowledge and skills in analysing data using system software in presenting business solutions effectively. This CmU will also provide opportunities for learners to build and enhance their knowledge in key digital transformations. Specifically, learners will be equipped with knowledge in relevant data structures, databases and cloud computing.


Financial Accounting & Interpretation [60 hours]

Employers are always looking for graduates who have good understanding of accounting concepts and practices to properly account for business transactions and assess business financial performance. In this CmU, learners will learn to apply accounting concepts and principles for the recording of business transactions, and the preparation of year-end adjustments and financial statements. In addition, learners will acquire competencies in interpreting financial statements and information for various stakeholders.


Managing Health & Wellness [45 hours]

The dynamic health and wellness industry demands that Sport & Wellness graduates are equipped with knowledge and competencies to identify wellness trends and apply targeted solutions to improve the wellness status of people from individual, community to societal levels. In this CmU, learners will learn the skills to conduct needs analysis based on the bio-psychosocial aspects of individuals and thereafter recommend wellness solutions to improve identified areas of concern. Learners will also learn the dimensions of health and wellness, transtheoretical change model, and behavioural change strategies to help improve overall body wellness status.


Professional Business Communication [60 hours]

Professional business communication that is driven in companies determine the success of their operations and business. Strong communication skills are needed in AI-driven and sustainable workplaces.  In this CmU, learners will master must-have business communication skills to achieve professional success. This CmU builds learners' communication competencies in applying effective business writing strategies and persuasive presentation techniques to influence and win over co-workers and customers in person and online. Learners will also sharpen their communication proficiencies to enhance personal branding and workplace relationships that advance careers. Learners will also master the art of business storytelling to better connect with audiences, especially for sustainability.


Sport & Exercise Science [45 hours]

In the sports & exercise industry, a firm foundation in human anatomy, biomechanics, and physiology is vital for sport practitioners. The knowledge of the principles of sport and exercise science along with the use of various health tools and fitness assessments are useful competencies to assess and evaluate health and fitness performance. Learners will also be equipped with knowledge to explain the structure and functions of human anatomy, physiology biomechanics and fitness assessment appraisal. This includes principles of skeleto-muscular injury management during exercise and sports, health and fitness appraisal, the energy systems that govern exercise and sports performance, and in conducting a biomechanic analysis on sport skill to optimise sport performance. Learners will develop competencies in the healthcare and sport science sector to provide solutions for sports performance and to assess obesity status amongst clients.


Sport Management & Governance [45 hours]

The sporting industry is a fully recognised globalised entity. It values sport management practitioners who are proficient in administration and who possess deep understanding of key concepts of sport promotion, high performance, community participation, delivery systems and facility usage, governance, sport business, risk management and sports industry development. Learners will examine processes by which sport policy is developed, explore the administrative frameworks for their feasibility, delivery and implementation, including a review of responsibilities and role of Government and National Sports Associations, and their management of athletes and talent. Learners will also examine the division of responsibilities between different sporting stakeholders and how these relationships are managed. They will also analyse the policy-making process for contemporary issues and the extent to which they achieve intended outcomes. There will be opportunities for learners to practise and experience various types of sport management functions as they evaluate, interpret and appreciate the implementation of Vision 2030 through guest lectures, case studies and field trips.


Strategic Business Communication [45 hours]

Companies engage in strategic business communication.  Skilled strategic communications cultivate positive relationships, promote inclusive social well-being, and safeguard their organisations' reputations through effective interpersonal and digital communication.  A strategic focus includes communicating sustainability effectively tailored to different circumstances and cultural contexts.  In this CmU, learners will demonstrate defining strategic communication skills to thrive in increasingly diverse, multicultural and digital workplaces. Specifically, learners will apply strategic and critical thinking that are part of strategic communication skills to achieve bias-free communication, foster inclusiveness and negotiate challenging exchanges in person or virtual.


User-Centred Design [45 hours]

Companies need to thrive in a business environment often termed as VUCA - volatile, uncertain, complex and ambiguous - and to survive and thrive in this world of disruption, the name of the game is "innovation". Companies value employees who can innovate and develop solutions that meet the needs of its users. This CmU will provide opportunities for learners to practise and experience the key stages of Design Thinking, such as being empathetic to users, identifying pain-points, generating creative ideas, prototyping and testing the innovations, as learners apply the various tools and methods through collaborative learning.

Data Science Fundamentals and Analytics [60 hours]

As businesses move toward a digitalised future, data will be gathered in ever greater quantity and variety. The skill to understand and make sense of data therefore becomes ever more important. In this CmU, learners will gain an awareness of the concepts of data science, with a specific focus on business analytics. Learners will be able to analyse data using exploratory data analytics through the application of data visualisation and will also be able to create a basic machine learning model for predictive analytics. These skills to organise and prepare data and apply data analysis techniques will allow learners to gain insights from data, aid business functions and support business decision making.


Fitness Training & Exercise Prescription [45 hours]

It is important for the fitness professional to be able to prescribe training programmes to both improve fitness performance and reduce injury risks as these are essential skills to bring value to the fitness gym and their clients. In this CmU, learners will be equipped with skillsets to conduct personal training and fitness services in both with and without the use of equipment. Learners will learn to create their pitch to sell fitness services, evaluate the performance gaps in their clientsâ€™ fitness, and programme the training based on periodisation and general adaptation syndrome. Learners will learn principles of periodisation to have training blocks customised with progressive overload in mind to help meet clients' training goals and while being mindful of any physical contraindications found during assessments. Learners will also learn to operate wearable digital technology to provide immediate feedback on the intensity of clients' physical training.


Health Coaching & Behaviour Modification [45 hours]

The awareness and interest in preventive health and wellness has enhanced the general well-being of society. This will drive the demand for individuals with preventive care consultation, coaching and behavioural modification skills. In this CmU, learners will be equipped with health coaching skills and techniques to identify health gaps and/or needs and facilitate behavioural changes to optimise health and wellness. Learners will also be taught psychological theories, goal setting techniques, programme design frameworks, counselling skills and behaviour modification techniques to help individuals lead healthier lifestyles.


Innovation & Entrepreneurship Project (WIU) [45 hours]

This WIU culminates the competencies learnt in Year 2 and 3. Learners are required to apply the knowledge and skills gathered previously to test the viability of a business in preparation to launch it.  Learners will adopt the Agile Scrum framework to develop the skills to validate business model using Design Thinking and Lean Business Model Canvas methodologies, execute prototype testing to determine Problem-Solution Fit and Minimum Viable Product (MVP) testing to determine Product-Market Fit. They will also develop entrepreneurial attributes such as perseverance, resilience and risk-taking. This WIU will also provide opportunities for coaching from experienced business innovators currently from the industry, funding opportunities for pre-incubation and adoption of the business plan by industry partners.


Innovation & Viable Business Development [45 hours]

Studies have shown that although products and service innovation are essential for the initial success of a business, business model innovation can deliver more lasting competitive advantage, particularly in this disruptive era. This CmU aims to develop learnersâ€™ capability in business model innovation by applying Lean Business Model Canvas (LBMC) methodology on a business case. By analysing the evolving business models in terms of the integrated components of LBMC, learners develop in-depth knowledge and skills in enhancing business competitive advantage and value creation.


Integrated Wellness Applications [45 hours]

The average life expectancy in Singapore has been increasing. According to a study by the University of Washington's Institute for Health Metrics and Evaluation (IHME) published on 17 Oct 2018 in the medical journal â€˜The Lancetâ€™, the 2040 forecasted average life expectancy in Singapore will be 85.4 years. With longer life expectancy, more and more individuals are proactively looking for ways to live a healthier lifestyle and keep diseases at bay. In this CmU, learners will be equipped with the knowledge and skills to enhance their personal health and wellness, and also those of their loved ones. Specifically, learners will learn hormetic methods to enhance health, anti-aging pathways as well as ways to moderate risk of diseases. Learners will have the opportunity to experiment on some of these techniques on themselves.


International Business Analysis [60 hours]

Globalisation has led to increased international trade. Many small and medium enterprises are joining the leagues of multinational companies to buy and sell overseas. Thus, it is important for business owners and investors to assess the attractiveness of new foreign markets.  This CmU will equip learners with fundamental competency in the identification of desirable foreign markets using environmental scanning and basic country analysis. In addition, learners will be taught various market entry strategies for potential expansion.


Managing Sport & Hybrid Events [45 hours]

Singaporeâ€™s ambition to be the hub for Singaporeâ€™s ambition to be the hub for hosting major international sports events not only brings economic benefits but also requires a spectrum of capabilities and services to create enjoyable and positive experiences for athletes, fans and viewers. In this CmU, learners will be equipped with the knowledge in the principles and processes of planning, managing and staging of sports and esports events, workforce development and building event legacies. They will attain competencies that are required in managing sports and esports events. In this digital age where Gen Z and millenial viewers are passionate about interactive and immersive multi-platform entertainment, learners will discover how streaming technology and digital channel broadcasting contribute to the innovation in media in esports and hybrid events.


Sport Marketing & Media [45 hours]

In a fast-paced and competitive sports industry, employers are looking for graduates who are not only proficient in technical sport knowledge but also in business application skills such as marketing in a sports context. In this CmU, learners will explore the complexities of sports as a multidimensional product that serves a vast variety of stakeholders. Learners will have the opportunity to examine a comprehensive background of the sport marketing industry with emphasis on branding, sponsorships and the availability of new media outlets.


Sports Coaching & Performance Analysis [45 hours]

In the modern sporting landscape, coaches play integral roles in the growth and development of holistic athletes at recreational and competitive levels. In this CmU, learners will acquire the fundamental knowledge and essential practices of sports coaching and be familiar with tools and applications to analyse skill movements and team performances. Learners will learn to develop training programmes, portfolios and establish good training practices for their athletes and participants using evidence-based practices. Through this CmU, learners will apply multi-dimensional concepts such as sports science, psychology, nutrition, physiology and life skills.


Sports Coaching Project (WIU) [30 hours]

Sports coaching in Singapore has become an immensely important part of Sport Singaporeâ€™s responsibility to achieve the sporting potential of athletes and participants. Coaches give their time and expertise to identify talent, develop skillsets and nurture potential. In this learning WIU, students analyse their development in sports coaching by applying the theoretical elements of sport science and integrate the scientific concepts and factors within sports performance. Through this WIU, learners will have the opportunity to assess their proficiency in performing and analysing sport practical activities through a Development Log and Individual/Dual Practice.


CmUs/WIUs in Year 3

  • Teaching Enterprise Project (Practicum) (WIU)
  • Internship Programme (WIU)

Teaching Enterprise Project (Practicum) (WIU) [840 hours]

In an evolving sporting landscape and a fervent wellness sector, employers are on the lookout for graduates with technical skills and competencies developed through practice-based and systems-oriented approaches. In this WIU, learners will learn to synthesise and put into practice acquired technical concepts and skills to perform multi-faceted tasks and projects in three main areas: sport events, fitness training and wellness management. Learners will work in teams to conceptualise, evaluate needs and analyse options before executing projects and events in line with industry standards. These include end-to-end delivery of sport events with event organisers, customising fitness assessments and fitness programmes for clients, and identifying wellness gaps and health coaching for all age groups. Through these interconnected tasks, learners will develop active work attitudes, can-do mindsets and team dynamism. This WIU aims to provide hands-on, real-world business scenarios for learners to apply the skills and knowledge acquired and achieve competencies relevant in the sport & wellness sectors.


Internship Programme (WIU) [960 hours]

Internship provides valuable work experiences and opportunities to develop learners' competencies in a real work environment. This internship programme comprises a 24- week attachment to a local or overseas business, public or governmental organisation. The attachment provides learners with real-life working experiences and develops in them the right work attitude, a practice-oriented and hands-on mindset, and the ability to work with diverse people in an organisation. The learners' performance during their attachment is graded. Participating organisations will provide a detailed training programme, which may include project assignments and/or rotational on-the-job training.
