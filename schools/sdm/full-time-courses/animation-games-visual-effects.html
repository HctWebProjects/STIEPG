About the Course

Get people of all ages hooked on your game! 

Are you fascinated by games or movies that capture your attention with compelling digital characters, animation or visual effects?

Join us on this exciting journey! Create visual worlds — from concept ideation to the final product — with imagery expressed through moving images and games.

Our comprehensive foundation modules will teach you broad skills in animation, games and visual effects to help you become tomorrow’s great media entertainers.

The Diploma in Animation, Games & Visual Effects is for you if you want to create engaging entertainment experiences through storytelling and play. 

Benefits

  • Develop expertise in look development, 2D and 3D animation, visual effects and media production.
  • Master the art of story creation and storytelling, and gain design-related skills in games, character art and environment.
  • Cultivate a broad range of skills and deepen your knowledge in the fields of animation, games or visual effects.
  • Be taught in world-class facilities by award-winning professionals who have worked at renowned companies such as Lucasfilm, The Walt Disney Company, Industrial Light & Magic, KOEI TECMO Singapore and Ubisoft.
  • Gain experience in working on real-life industry projects. 
  • Earn industry certifications from our partners like Epic Games in addition to your diploma.
  • Look forward to exciting careers with top animation and VFX companies such as Lucasfilm Animation and Industrial Light & Magic, or top game companies such as Ubisoft and Bandai Namco.

NYP Professional Competency Model (NYP-PCM) - its unique features

This diploma will be taught using the NYP-PCM starting AY2023.

Industry Relevance

Endorsed by industry leaders like Ubisoft Singapore, MediaCorp TV & Infinite Studios, it is informed by real industry intelligence and needs.

Professional Accreditation

You gain additional industry certifications while you complete your diploma, putting you ahead of the pack.

Agile Response

The NYP Professional Competency Model for the Diploma in Animation, Games & Visual Effects consists of:

  • 22 Competency Units – Within each interdisciplinary Competency Unit, skills and knowledge from different disciplines are integrated and taught holistically.  This enables the students to fulfil specific work-tasks upon completion. Completing the Competency Units will also equip our students with five Course Competencies.
  • Four Competency Canvases  –  where skills and knowledge learnt are assessed for competency, together with a Work-Integration-Unit (WIU) which is a workplace contextualised project. You have to complete all four to graduate.
  • One Final Year Project (FYP).
  • One Internship Programme (ITP).
  • 11 General Studies Modules (GSMs) – these modules allow you to explore other areas of interest beyond your chosen area of specialisation.

Students enrolled in Diploma in Animation, Games & Visual Effects under the Professional Competency Model will have to complete 22 Competency Units (CmUs). Within each interdisciplinary CmU, skills and knowledge from different disciplines are integrated and taught holistically, enabling students to fulfil specific worktasks upon completion.

Completing the CmUs will also equip our students with Four Course Competencies (CCs) below.

  • 2D Art
  • Storytelling
  • 3D Art
  • Real-Time Technology

and one of the following three elective course competencies:

  • Animation
  • Games
  • Visual Effects

 

Students will also need to complete Work-Integration-Unit (WIU) which can be in the form of an internship or project that mirror work situations where students can demonstrate their skills and competencies.

  • Drawing & Rendering
  • Design for Communication
  • Storytelling Through Media

  • Figure Drawing
  • 2D Animation

Design Exploration [60 hours]

Design Exploration is an essential process for all learners to perform at the beginning of every project to influence the design direction and it’s (project) outcome. Learners will demonstrate how they make their decisions through collaborative tasks in observational research. They will demonstrate how they make connections to form creative ideas that may be applicable in different design disciplines by analysing the data that they have collected.


Drawing & Rendering [60 hours]

Visual communication is one of the most effective ways of conveying information as the cognitive function of the human brain processes images and graphics faster. This CmU allows learners to apply elements of drawing to visualise, draw shapes and forms using various media and techniques. Through this CmU, learners will gain essential perceptual drawing skills including perspective. They will ideate and communicate effectively through sketching and exploration.


Design for Communication [60 hours]

Design for communication is an essential primer to introduce principles of design and visual elements that apply to 2D and 3D design. Learners will effectively apply the use of colour theory, grids, gestalt principles and typography as integral elements of the visual design created. They will use imagery through collages to create meaning as a form of communication. Learners will also use digital tools in manipulating imagery and artwork.


Storytelling Through Media [60 hours]

Storytelling in the context of Media is important and utilise fundamental tools required to create compelling stories. Through this CmU, learners will harness essential narrative tools such as story structure, visual aesthetics, and dramatic techniques to incite audience engagement and interest. To apply, learners will create and communicate their own stories to audiences.


 


User-Centred Studio Practice (WIU) [60 hours]

User-centred design helps to understand users’ needs and preferences regarding features of a product, task, goals, user flows. etc and thus improving user experience. In this WIU, learners will communicate and collaborate in teams to gather user requirements, identify opportunities, apply the design process and prototype a creative solution for the user.


Figure Drawing [60 hours]

In Figure Drawing, the observational study of the human body, its anatomical structure and proportion are essential to creating aesthetics, styles and realism in drawing. Through this CmU, learners will gain a deeper understanding and appreciation for the human figure, its anatomical structure and body language to be effective artists. They will develop essential traits like observation, continuous practice and personal development as part of their learning journey.


2D Animation [60 hours]

The principles of animation are essential to creating believable movements of characters and objects in animation production. Through this CmU, the learners will be competent in applying animation principles such as motion, timing, weight, overlapping actions and follow-throughs in their 2-dimensional drawings to create the illusion of life in inanimate objects and characters.


Digital Painting [30 hours]

Digital Painting is a crucial component for digital media production as it requires the artistry and aesthetics required to make the production look visually appealing and polished. Through this CmU, learners will gain useful technical skills and knowledge of concepts and elements of painting such as: value, colour, lighting and composition. They will be given the opportunities to synthesise their digital painting skills with texture creation and rendering as part of the industry practice for animation production workflow.


Storyboard & Animatics [60 hours]

Storyboarding is a crucial function in media productions. By considering the technical methodology and creative aspects in the evolutionary nature of storyboarding, storyboard is an essential tool to connect with audience in the media industries. Through this CmU, learners will be competent to communicate ideas and emotions through the production and pitching of storyboard and animatics.


3D Workflow [60 hours]

For effective 3D production to take place, it requires good knowledge of the 3D production processes such as modelling that plays a crucial role in translating the 2D concepts into 3D form, animation, lighting and texturing.  Through this CmU, the learners will gain a better understanding of the 3D production processes within animation, games and visual effects pipeline as well as become more competent in creating hard surface modelling and organic modelling to create geometries that can be textured, lighted, rendered for image-making, animation and visual effects.


Pre-Visualisation Production (WIU) [60 hours]

Pre-visualization is an essential stage in the digital media production as it provides a preview of the written content in visual form. In this WIU, learners will demonstrate their competency in creating an original content based on the concepts of storyboarding and the principles of animation that will provide industry realism in their pre-production planning. During this process, learners will also enhance their ability in communicating and collaborating in a team in order to pitch their ideas from conception to implementation.

Visual Development [60 hours]

Visual Development plays a critical role in the pre-production stage of the digital media production. Learners will pick up methods to translate story ideas into visual designs and develop a keen sense of aesthetics. Through this CmU, learners will become competent in art direction and develop good designs for character, props, sets, environment, colour, compositing and lighting.


3D Asset Creation [60 hours]

In 3D Asset Creation, learners will get to create 3D visuals and assets for digital media productions while learning to adhere to technical specifications and limitations. Learners will explore the aesthetics, visual concepts as well as the technicalities and optimisation requirements for various 3D applications that are crucial in the development pipeline. Through this CmU, learners will be competent to construct, edit, and integrate 3D assets for a variety of entertainment platforms.


3D Animation [60 hours]

The application of the principles of animation is crucial to create smooth and believable movements of the subject in a digital media production. Through this CmU, the learners will be competent in dealing with the concepts of timing and motion of the subject matter by applying the principles of animation and animation techniques in the 3-dimensional space.


Character Animation [60 hours]

The application of pantomime animation is crucial to create believable movements and realistic performances of the subject in a digital media production. Through this CmU, the learners will be competent in acting, timing and motion of the subject matter by applying body mechanics and animation techniques in motion capture and multi-dimensional spaces.


Digital Sculpting [30 hours]

Modelling plays a crucial role in translating the 2D concepts into 3D models.  Depending on the complexity of the 2D concepts and technical specifications, the application of the advance modelling techniques and optimalisation techniques may be required. Through this CmU, learners will be competent in creating organic modelling such as human, animal and fantasy creatures as well as low-polygon modelling for real-time animation and 3D prints through the use of digital sculpting tools and application of optimalisation techniques.


Project Management [30 hours]

Project resources such as time and manpower are often scarce. As such, it is important to maximise the productivity and efficiency of production pipeline by applying effective project management techniques. Through this CmU, learners will be competent to manage various resources and work both independently and collaboratively to deliver quality products.


Principles of Game Design [60 hours]

The success of a game often lies in its game design. By applying various game design principles such as the MDA framework, an entertaining interactive experience can be delivered to the players. Through this CU, learners will be competent to design effective and ethical gameplay, perform in-game analysis, and deliver a pitch for their game design.


Film Production [60 hours]

Cinematography creates and supports the overall look and mood of a film’s visual narrative. The cinematography techniques include the knowledge of scripting, camera placement, framing, lighting, image composition and aesthetics. Through this CmU, the learners will develop a solid framework in cinematography and key techniques in filmmaking as applied in the visual effects industry.


Dynamic Simulations [30 hours]

Dynamic Simulations is an integral process of discovering the complexities of effects simulation in the production pipeline.  Many of the best VFX artists are highly competent in this field of effects simulation.  In this CmU, learners will develop competencies in computational problem-solving and creating stylised or realistic visual effects. They will also be competent in crating particle dynamics, rigid body, soft body and other dynamics using 3D applications.


Cinematic Production (WIU) [60 hours]

Cinematic is a powerful tool that aims to attract and engage the audience. In this WIU, learners will synthesise their acquired technical skills and knowledge to create exciting visuals for a team-based production. In addition, learners will enhance their ability in pitching the concept, collaborating with peers and production planning that will bring the concept to realisation.


2D Asset Creation [60 hours]

To generate 2D visuals and assets, learners need to observe technical specifications and limitations in order to perform digital media productions. Across the concept, design and production phases, learners need to employ various tools and techniques to ensure optimal aesthetic quality and efficiency. Through this CmU, learners will be competent to develop and integrate 2D assets for a variety of entertainment platforms.


Lighting, Texturing & Rendering [60 hours]

Lighting, Texturing and Rendering is an essential component in enhancing the aesthetics and creating a realistic or stylised imagery in digital media production. Through this CmU, learners will be competent to produce realistic or stylised images by applying the design principles and using the various tools and techniques in the 3D application.


Compositing [30 hours]

Compositing has a powerful role in film and digital media production as it enhances the aesthetics, style, realism and finishing of the moving imagery. Through this CmU, learners will be competent in digital imaging, compositing and producing high quality image output after learning the various tools and techniques to combine the various live action sequences, images and/or 3D elements.


Rigging [30 hours]

Rigging plays a significant role in bringing motion to 3D models. The complexity in the construction of the digital skeleton will determine the level of editing and customisation in the animation of the 3D model. Through this CmU, the learners will be competent in rigging props and characters while working around constraints and achieving optimisation in a media production.


Look Development [60 hours]

Look development is an essential part of the pre-production phase as it establishes the overall artistic and style of the film. While deciding on the art direction, the knowledge of lighting, materials shading and surface volume is required. Through this CmU, learners will become competent in building the materials' shading, surface volume and the application of point by applying instantiation principles and techniques.


Business for Digital Media Industry [30 hours]

Starting a business involves developing ideas and materialising them. In this CmU, learners will develop the competencies of polishing a product idea to attract interest from stakeholders as well as how to raise start-up funds to get their business started. It will also equip learners with the competency of understanding intellectual properties matters for their business. Digital media entrepreneurs will also be invited to share their experiences to give learners an insight to the media industry.


Level Design [60 hours]

Design of worlds, levels, missions and quests is important to provide an engaging user experience. Players will lose interest if the level progression is made too difficult while making it too simple will result in lower players’ satisfaction. In this CmU, learners will develop the competencies in designing game worlds, levels, missions, quests and controls based on level design principles, asset preparation, and playability assessments for 2D and 3D games.


VFX Cinematography [60 hours]

By applying visual effects elements in filmmaking, learners will be given exposure to advanced film setup and on-set visual effects supervision. Learners will gain technical knowledge on how the camera captures information in relation to the elements of the on-set location. Through this CmU, learners will develop the competencies in capturing a scene for computer graphics integration using various techniques and solutions such as green/blue screen, setting measurements, marker placement and production setups.


 


Procedural Effects [60 hours]

For the study of Procedural Effects, the ability to automatically generate effects in real-time to allow for a more diverse series of actions is essential in the digital media production pipeline.  Many of the best VFX artists are highly competent in this field.  In this CmU, learners will develop competencies in synthesising advanced procedural effects techniques to create and render visual effects, such as dynamic destruction, debris generation, cloth and fluid simulation. Learners will create shot-based effects using physical references to achieve realistic visuals.


Specialised Media Production (WIU) [60 hours]

Media productions, such as films and games, entertain the modern audience through different platforms and devices every day. Proficiency in various disciplines is called upon to produce an entertainment product that can engage and captivate the masses. In this WIU, learners will enhance their competency in generating ideas and assets for specified digital media productions while considering production pipeline, collaborating with peers and pitching the product to an audience.

  • Independent Work Project (WIU)

Independent Work Project (WIU) [180 hours]

This WIU provides learners with an opportunity to work on a self-initiated or client-based project that adds value to their portfolio in at least one of the areas from the design and media domain. Through this WIU, learners will synthesize both knowledge and skills learnt throughout the course and deploy different learning approaches for acquiring new knowledge and skills as required by the project. They will also communicate clearly their project ideas, project progress and final product delivery through various pitches, progress updates and milestone presentations. This can help learners build their personal branding for future job applications.


Portfolio Development (WIU) [60 hours]

Portfolio is a very crucial element that showcases the artist's strength and skillsets. In this WIU, learners will be competent to produce a professional portfolio to showcase their best works that will exceed the industry expectation. It also prepares learners to present their portfolio to potential employers.


Procedural Asset Creation [30 hours]

To generate 3D assets for digital media production, learners will need to follow technical specifications and optimisation requirements for various applied 3D platforms. Through this CmU, learners will be competent in creating procedural assets that are easily accessible to digital artists, through the design of user interfaces and extensions for existing modelling and animation tools.


Real-time Engine [30 hours]

As the media industry continues to evolve, real-time technology has emerged as the leading trend in content production for various entertainment platforms. By utilising real-time engines, 3D production workflow can be sped up by cutting down rendering time. Through this CmU, learners will be competent to utilise real-time engines and associated tools to create, edit, and interact with CG environments and objects.


Virtual Production [30 hours]

An overview of the digital media production pipeline can be achieved by virtual production technology. By applying and editing 3D assets using real-time CG applications, learners will be able to produce real-time output. Through this CmU, learners will be competent to film live action performers interacting with 3D scenes in a live virtual production background.


Immersive Media Technology [30 hours]

The merging of the physical world with a digital or simulated reality can be achieved by the use of immersive media technology. By applying principles of user experience design to extended reality environments, user engagement can be brought to a higher level. Through this CmU, learners will be able to design interactive extended reality experience through prototyping.


Internship Programme (WIU) [480 / 960 hours]

Through this Internship Programme, learners will be assigned to real-life workplace environments to receive On-the-Job Training (OJT) where they can apply the knowledge and skills acquired in the course. The learners will be guided by the supervisors or mentors at the workplace to develop the relevant competencies at the same time their learning will also be facilitated by school internship mentors throughout the internship period. This WIU will allow learners to develop competencies and experiences at the workplace. In addition, they will acquire important work values and ethics which include being responsible and positive, as well as taking initiative and exercising integrity. Through this internship programme, learners will be industry and future-ready.


 


Final Year Project (WIU) [480 / 960 hours]

This WIU requires learners to integrate and consolidate the principles and applicable skills learnt throughout the course. Learners will be tasked to complete different types of projects, which may involve collaboration with industry partners. Learners are expected to present their work at various development milestones and will be given feedback to help them improve their processes and outcomes.
