About the Course

Discover the world of food!

Diploma in Food Science & Nutrition is for you if you want to:

  • Improve the well-being of the community through the development of safe, innovative and nutritious food products.
  • Learn how food ingredients interact to create fascinating flavours and textures, while ensuring food safety, traceability and security.
  • Discover the wonders of science in food and nutrition, experiment with popular food for healthier makeover, and deploy technologies to ensure food security.
  • Gain relevant knowledge and skills, and keep abreast of industry trends and needs in our competency-based curriculum.

Benefits

  • Experience an interdisciplinary course that encompasses chemistry in food through ingredient interaction, biology in nutrition, microbiology and food safety, as well as physics in food processing.Â This course is accredited by the International Union of Food Science & Technology (IUFoST) for having met international guidelines and education standards.
  • Be mentored by lecturers with deep industry experience and make your mark in local and international competitions.
  • Be trained in state-of-the-art and industry-standard food pilot plants (including Singaporeâ€™s first pilot plant for beverage production) and ISO17025-accredited Food Safety Centre.
  • Gain opportunities to work in food-related local and multinational companies, or pursue further studies at universities in Singapore, Australia or the United Kingdom.

Course Structure

Students enrolled in Diploma in Food Science & Nutrition under the Professional Competency Model will have to complete 25 Competency Units (CmUs). Within eachÂ interdisciplinary CmU, skills and knowledge from different disciplines are integrated and taught holistically, enabling students to fulfil specific worktasks upon completion.

Completing the CmUs will also equip our students with the five Course Competencies (CCs) below.

  • Food Processing
  • Food Safety Management & Regulatory Affairs
  • Nutrition Strategy & Intervention
  • Product Innovation
  • Quality Control

Students will also need to complete Work-Integration-Unit (WIU)Â which can be in the form of an internship and/or project that mirror work situations where students can demonstrate their skills and competencies.

Applied Calculus in Scientific Processes [60 hours]

Scientific processes related to reaction rates, cell growth, radioactive decay, process optimisation and drug absorption are regularly monitored to ensure the efficiency of these processes when being used in applied science industry. Through this competency unit, learners will acquire knowledge and skills in differentiation and integration required for monitoring of various scenarios related to these scientific processes. In addition, learners will be introduced to the concept of exponential and logarithm functions to scaffold their learning for performing differentiation and integration of exponential and logarithmic expressions.


Biochemical Analysis [60 hours]

Biochemical analysis skills are essential in applied science laboratories so that one can perform the experiments accurately and safely. Learners will be equipped with concepts in biology and the processes that occur within a living system. In addition, learners will acquire skills in handling biological equipment and performing experiments according to standard protocols and biosafety standards.  


Chemical Laboratory Skills [60 hours]

Chemical laboratory skills are vital in applied science to obtain accurate and precise results while performing experiments safely. Through this competency unit, learners will acquire chemical laboratory skills and apply concepts of inorganic and physical chemistry to understand chemical reactions and analytical techniques. They will also be able to select suitable equipment for laboratory usage, plan and conduct experiments, tabulate experimental data and perform calculations to conclude the experiment.


Data & Statistical Methods [60 hours]

Statistical methods are important for processing and analysing data obtained from various studies in applied science. In this competency unit, learners will acquire the skill of quantitative reasoning for use in the decision-making through collecting, organizing, analysing, interpreting and presenting data.


Instruments & Measurements [45 hours]

The ability to operate instruments and obtain measurements are fundamental to applied science. This competency unit equips learners with concepts of physical quantities that are commonly used for process monitoring such as pressure, flow rate, temperature and heat. Learners will then develop the competencies to perform calculations and measurements of these quantities. 


Applied Science Project (WIU) [30 hours]

Applied Science skills are important in providing solutions for the real-world problems. This Work Integration Unit provides a platform for the learners to develop innovative scientific solutions to the problems identified, while developing innovative & enterprising (I&E) attributes for their career and life.


Food Hygiene Practice [30 hours]

Food hygiene practice is essential to the production and development of safe food products.  This competency unit will equip learners with the knowledge and practical skills to monitor and manage hygiene practices within the food industry according to Singapore Food Authority (SFA) regulations. Learners will be competent to take on food safety officer roles to conduct internal hygiene checks according to SFA food premise licence requirement.


Macronutrients Analysis [60 hours]

Food analysis provides valuable information about chemical composition, processing, quality control and contamination of food stuff, ensuring compliance with food regulations. This competency unit equips learners with the essential knowledge and practical skills in food chemistry. Learners will acquire organic chemistry knowledge necessary in the application of food macromolecules such as water, carbohydrates, proteins and lipids. Learners will be competent in conducting chemical and instrumental analysis of these macromolecules and present these findings in the form of a scientific report which includes data analysis and oral technical presentation skills.


Microbial Cultivation & Control [60 hours]

Microbiological skills and techniques are essential to personnel working in food quality control to ensure food safety. Through this competency unit, learners will be equipped with knowledge on various groups of microorganisms, factors affecting their growth and the methods of cultivation.  They will also develop the ability to determine general populations of microbes in foods through laboratory exercises.


Nutrition Interpretation [60 hours]

Interpretating nutrition is an important skill to ensure that nutrition intervention is administered appropriately. Through this competency unit, learners will be proficient in nutrition applications of anatomy and physiology, as well as biochemistry in the human body. Besides being competent in carrying out a nutritional assessment with the knowledge gained, they will also have a good grasp of how caloric and non-caloric nutrients are being digested, processed, and utilized in the human body. 


Sensory Evaluation [30 hours]

The understanding of the sensory quality of food products and various sensory evaluation methods used in conducting consumer testing is critical in the process of food product development. Learners will develop competency to create, conduct and interpret results of various sensory tests using knowledge gained from sensory evaluation principles and theories.


Unit Operations [30 hours]

Unit operations are important processes in the conversion of raw materials to consumable food products. This competency unit equips learners with the knowledge of engineering principles and unit operations widely used in the food industry. Learners will be competent to perform determinations of various unit operations such as unit conversions, fluid flow, heat and mass transfer vital in the food manufacturing processes.

Diagnostics in Food Microbiology [60 hours]

Identification of hygiene indicator and pathogenic microorganisms is a vital part of food microbiology to ensure consumer safety. Through this competency unit, learners will be equipped with key microbiological principles in food spoilage, foodborne diseases, microbiological sampling and testing, and current trends in diagnostic microbiology. Learners will also develop analytical skills critical for working in a microbiology laboratory using various conventional and advanced methods in determining specific microorganisms in food, as well as gain insights of artificial intelligence approaches used in these advanced methods.


Food Ingredient Applications [60 hours]

Food ingredients are indispensable in food applications and new product development. Through this competency unit, learners are equipped with knowledge on the attributes, functions and applications of various types of food ingredients/additives commonly used in food industry. Learners will be capable to conduct laboratory studies involving these ingredients/additives needed for new product development and product re-formulation. Learners will be competent working on regulatory compliance and as prospective sales personnel in food application business.


Food Regulation Management [45 hours]

Food regulation ensures food safety and that consumers have the information needed to make an informed decision. In this competency unit, learners will acquire the skills to interpret and evaluate local and international food regulations for various manufactured food products. This is essential for food personnel working in food product development and food safety management. In addition, learners will develop competencies in designing food labels that comply with relevant regulations.  Learners will also investigate the compliance of various food to health and nutrition claims for both food manufacturing and food service sectors.


Food Service Technology [60 hours]

Food service technology innovations are creating new food processing techniques that can be used in the food service sector to satisfy dinersâ€™ expectation. These techniques provide competitive edge to food personnel working in the development of novel food dishes.   Learners will acquire knowledge on traditional and non-traditional cooking methods to accomplish desirable sensorial attributes such as appearance, texture and taste for diners.


Micronutrients & Trace Elements Analysis [60 hours]

Micronutrients and trace elements analysis is vital in laboratories that provide consultancy, research and development (R&D) or quality assurance services in the food manufacturing R&D and quality control sectors. Through this competency unit, learners will gain essential knowledge and analytical skills in micronutrients and trace elements analyses. Learners would develop competency in using advanced chromatography techniques (GC-MS) and spectrophotometric techniques (UV/VIS) to measure the presence of trace elements and determine micronutrients content in food and environmental samples. They would acquire insightful knowledge to the different chemical reactions within food systems that are caused by enzymatic and non-enzymatic factors, as well as the impact from food additives. 


Nutrition Planning [30 hours]

Nutrition Planning is an important skill to ensure that individuals obtain adequate nutrition to support normal growth and development at different life stages, from preconception, through pregnancy, birth, infancy, childhood, adolescence and adulthood into aging. Learners will be proficient in planning a meal plan to address the nutrition problems of an individual, whilst taking cultural and socioeconomic factors into consideration. Learners will be able to create and analyse several types of menus used in the food and nutrition industries.


Food Packaging [30 hours]

Food packaging is an essential component of the food industry, assuring the safe handling and delivery of both fresh and processed food products. Through this competency unit, learners will gain knowledge of various food packaging materials and packaging systems as well as their applications in shelf-life extension of food products. Learners will develop competency to provide advice on the suitable packaging material or system to ensure product quality and food safety based on the sustainability initiatives in the food industry.


Food Safety Management System [45 hours]

Food safety management is critical in reducing risk of foodborne illness and ensuring the safest products possible. In this competency unit, learners will be equipped with essential knowledge in quality management system, good manufacturing practices, food safety management system and international certifications used in food establishment. Learners will also acquire skills to perform risk assessment, management and communication. In addition, learners will develop a HACCP plan with considerations of key milestones to assure safety of food by actively controlling risks and hazards throughout the food process.


Food Technology [60 hours]

Food technology such as food processing and preservation techniques are essential in the research and development of food products. These techniques enable learners to scale up their bench top creation to a safe and high-quality manufacturing volume. Learners will gain knowledge of food spoilage causes and apply food processing and preservations techniques to extend the shelf-life of food products. Learners will also acquire competency to implement Good Manufacturing Practices in food production and be able to keep abreast with current food processing trends.


Therapeutic Nutrition [60 hours]

Therapeutic nutrition knowledge is essential in preventing and managing nutrition-related conditions. This competency unit will equip learners with necessary knowledge on nutrition management in various settings. Learners will also acquire skills in nutrition assessment that leads to therapeutic nutrition intervention through relevant case discussions. 


Food Safety Analysis Project (WIU) [60 hours]

Food safety analysis is important in food safety testing laboratories and food companies to ensure products are safe. In this work integration unit, learners will apply food safety and microbiological skills to determine the common types of food safety analysis required for different products and to perform microbiological, chemical and/or physical analyses. Learners will also acquire skills to interpret test results against customers or regulatory requirements.


Therapeutic Diet Design Project (WIU) [60 hours]

Therapeutic diet design is essential part of nutrition care process for individuals with nutrition-related conditions. This work integration unit will equip learners with knowledge and skills in therapeutic diet design in various settings. Learners will also acquire skills in basic therapeutic diet plan and development through relevant practical sessions.

Food Business & Marketing [75 Hours]

Food business and marketing is a key stage of product development to bring product to consumer. In this competency unit, learners will interpret needs of users with an empathy tool to ideate product concepts. Learners will develop marketing plans and deploy test marketing exercises with consideration of key milestones and goals. Through the application of a start-up tool, learners will evaluate the viability of the product idea to start-up their business as an entrepreneur or join a food company as a food technologist or marketing executive.


Food Product Innovation [45 hours]

The development of new food products is an important part of a business strategy in food establishment as it helps to recruit new consumers, increase product sales and market share, and improve consumer satisfaction. A successful new product development uses appropriate technical solutions to meet market demands and needs. Through this competency unit, learners will be able to execute the complete innovation process using suitable project management approaches. Learners will be able to conceptualise and formulate a new product from information gathered through brainstorming and consumer feedback.  Learners will also become proficient in presenting product prototype, packaging, product specification and nutritional label for a new product launch.


Food Security & Sustainability [60 hours]

Food security and sustainability is crucial to feed the global population and minimize the effects of climate change. This competency unit equips learners with knowledge related to food security and sustainability as they discover more about how food and agriculture industries respond and adapt to changes in the environment. Learners will also gain perspectives on how global issues such as the change in population and land usage affect food security and sustainability. Learners will develop competencies to combat food wastage by analysing the properties of food waste and convert it into value-added product which is handy in food manufacturing.


Nutrition Service & Applications [60 hours]

Nutrition service and application are essential aspects of contemporary nutrition.  This competency unit will equip learners with essential knowledge and skills in implementing nutrition service and evidence-based applications in the sports arena, pharmaceutical industry, commercial world, community, school and healthcare system. Learners will also be skilled in execution of nutrition services through ethical and evidence-based approaches in various settings.


Product Concept & Development Project (WIU) [75 hours]

New food product development is essential for companies to stay competitive and satisfy consumer demand. In this work integration unit, learners will experience the role of a Food Product Developer who will create new food product prototypes and formulate recipes to meet the needs of target consumers. Learners will apply their knowledge on food ingredients and processing, food regulations, analytical methods, sensory evaluation, and marketing to develop a new food product safe and pleasing for consumers.


Internship Programme (WIU) [480/ 960 hours]

Through this Internship Programme, learners will be assigned to real-life workplace environments to receive On-the-Job Training (OJT) where they can apply the knowledge and skills acquired in the course. The learners will be guided by the supervisors or mentors at the workplace to develop the relevant competencies at the same time their learning will also be facilitated by school internship mentors throughout the internship period. This will allow learners to develop competencies and experiences at the workplace. In addition, they will acquire important work values and ethics which include being responsible and positive, as well as taking initiative and exercising integrity. Through this internship programme, learners will be industry and future-ready.


Overseas Internship Programme (WIU) [960 hours]

Through this Internship Programme, learners will be assigned to real-life workplace environments to receive On-the-Job Training (OJT) where they can apply the knowledge and skills acquired in the course. The learners will be guided by the supervisors or mentors at the workplace to develop the relevant competencies at the same time their learning will also be facilitated by school internship mentors throughout the internship period. This will allow learners to develop competencies and experiences at the workplace. In addition, they will acquire important work values and ethics which include being responsible and positive, as well as taking initiative and exercising integrity. Through this internship programme, learners will be industry and future-ready.


Final Year Project (WIU) [480 hours]

Industry-linked projects provide an excellent platform for practical applications of skills and knowledge. Learners will be competent to undertake assigned projects with well-defined objectives that meet industrial standards. The projects are designed to cultivate independent learning. Learners will apply creative thinking in problem-solving and practise their communication skills in presenting and explaining their concepts to the target audience.
