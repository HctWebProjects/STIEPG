About the Course

Unlock a dynamic career with Diploma in Electronic and Computer Engineering! Join the Smart Nation of Singapore with versatile skills gained from the course, partake in digital transformation moves and integrate smart technologies for diverse industries.

Explore the dynamic intersection of Electronics and Computer Engineering and delve into core principles of digital systems, microcontrollers, and software programming. Uncover the latest advancements in hardware design and software integration. What sets this course apart is the opportunity for students to earn industry certifications from leading companies, equipping them with tangible skills for the workforce. Gain hands-on experience, stay abreast of cutting-edge technologies, and position yourself for success in high-growth industries and sectors such as electronics, Infocomm, semiconductor, telecommunications and Internet of Things.

Algebra [60 hours]

Learners will study the basic mathematical principles and tools necessary to build their engineering foundation. They will apply mathematical methods, tools and notations proficiently in the analysis and solution of engineering problems. Topics covered include mathematical functions (polynomials, exponential, logarithms, step functions and trigonometry), complex numbers, determinants, matrices and vectors.


Calculus [60 hours]

Through this foundation module, learners will study essential knowledge in calculus and acquire skills for solving engineering problems encountered in their course of study. Topics include differentiation and integration of commonly used functions in engineering applications.


Digital Electronics [60hrs]

This module covers digital concepts and principles, which include binary and hexadecimal number systems, logic levels and logic devices, combinational and sequential logic. Learners will study the techniques in analysing, troubleshooting, designing and developing digital systems. Case studies are used to illustrate the applications of digital systems. Knowledge acquired in this module is essential for understanding the design and working of electronics-enabled systems and products.


Effective Communication Skills [30 hours]

Communicating well is a vital life skill, benefitting all aspects of our lives from professional life to social gatherings. It is thus important to be proficient in both written and oral communication. This module will enable learners to communicate effectively in different settings and on different collaborative platforms. There will be opportunities to practise and deliver various types of presentations, to learn to competently handle questions, and to effectively incorporate verbal and non-verbal elements when speaking. Learners will also acquire media and information literacy and be able to produce clearly written and well-structured reports and proposals, following standard citation and referencing guidelines.


Electrical Principles [60 hours]

This module covers electrical fundamentals and their applications in electrical and electronic circuits. Topics covered include principles of electricity, resistive circuits, magnetism and electromagnetism, inductive and capacitive circuits, and DC & AC sources. Students will need this knowledge to analyse, interpret and solve engineering problems.


Engineering Exploration Project [30 hours]

In this module, learners will be engaged in a systematic process of design thinking. Through this process, they will acquire innovative and enterprising skills to develop creative and technically robust solutions to real-world challenges.


Fundamentals of Innovation and Enterprise [30 hours]

Learners will develop attributes that are pervasive and synonymous with being innovative and enterprising for career and life. Through this module learners will develop positive practices when working with data, propose ideas using user-centric approaches and design processes, determine and locate resources, and leverage collaborative practices to formulate solutions.


Fundamentals of Mechanics [60 hours]

This module provides learners with the fundamentals of engineering mechanics. Topics covered include Newton laws, statics of rigid bodies, moment of inertia and bending moments. Fundamentals in dynamics that include kinematics and kinetics of particles will also be covered in this module. Learners will acquire fundamental engineering competency to understand and perform engineering-related activities related to statics and dynamics.


Internet and Web Development [60 hours]

Learners will gain essential knowledge and skills in developing effective front-end web applications through the design and development of web pages using HTML mark-up language and the CSS styling language. Learners will acquire the techniques in creating responsive web design for multi-form factor devices. An overview of internet infrastructure and its underlying technologies is also covered in this module.


Introduction to Engineering [60 hours]

In this module, learners are exposed to different engineering disciplines by focusing on the application of relevant core engineering disciplines. Topics covered include roles and responsibilities of an engineer, ethics, and sustainability. Learners will engage in the practice of engineering through the process of conceiving, designing, and implementing solutions to engineering problems, individually and in teams. Through this, they will also be able to hone their creative thinking and problem-solving skills, build synergistic teamwork and enhance their communication skills.


Programming [60 hours]

The practice-oriented module introduces learners to software development methodology. Learners will study problem-analysis and problem-solving techniques to develop essential programming skills. They will be able to create flowcharts, develop algorithms, write structured programs, test and debug programs.


Electronic Devices and Applications [60 hours]

In this module, learners will acquire the knowledge of electronic devices and their applications. Topics include semiconductor devices such as diodes, special-purpose diodes, bipolar junction transistors, field-effect transistors and operational amplifiers. The design and operations of the devices and their applications are covered. Learners will apply this knowledge to analyze and solve electronic circuit problems.


Workplace Digital Skills [30 hours]

In this module, learners will use word processing software to create, modify and style document/report effectively and efficiently. Learners will use spreadsheets to manage, organise and model data as well as to perform analysis and report generation. Creation of customised, dynamic and multimedia-style presentations using software tools will also be covered. Learners will gain and apply these skills through hands-on practices in preparation for the digital workplace.


General Studies

To provide you an all-rounded education, NYP offers General Studies Modules (GSMs). So besides taking modules related directly to your chosen diploma course, you will get exposed to areas beyond your usual field of study. Through a combination of prescribed and elective inter-disciplinary GSMs, you will gain interesting, practical knowledge that can be beneficial to you as you prepare for the world of work and life after graduation.

Differential Equation & Series [60 hours]

This module provides learners with the knowledge of solving ordinary differential equations and representing periodic functions in terms of Fourier series. The learners will be able to select and use appropriate models and techniques to solve engineering related problems. The main topics covered include first-order and second-order homogenous differential equations, Fourier series and its application.


Electric Circuit Analysis [60 hours]

This module aims to develop student's ability to analyse DC and AC electrical circuits. Topics include techniques of circuit analysis including mesh current and node voltage methods, Thevenin's and Norton's network theorems, and sinusoidal steady-state analysis. Learners will need this knowledge and skills to analyse and troubleshoot electric circuits.


Computer Networking [60 hours]

This module aims to equip learners with a comprehensive understanding coupled with practical hands-on in communication and computer networking fundamentals by referencing the Open Systems Interconnect (OSI) seven-layer model and the TCP/IP model. Coverage includes the complete network data encapsulation process, Ethernet networks, IP addressing schemes and basic routing concepts. The focus is on how networking concepts and technologies can be applied, configured and integrated in home and branch networking environments.


Application Programming [60 hours]

This module provides the foundation for software application development based on rapid application development techniques and object-oriented programming concepts. Using widely deployed industry development tools and programming framework, the module will cover programming language syntax, structures, concepts, techniques and industry best practises in application development. Learners will acquire the necessary skills to develop and deploy event-driven and GUI-based applications.


Connected System Design Project [90 hours]

This module is designed to introduce the key design considerations, concepts and technologies that are essential to the development of connected system which is central to Internet of Things (IoT) applications. Learners will apply their knowledge in electronics, computer, communications and software programming into the development of a connected system. Learners will also be able to implement projects using open source software and hardware.


Statistical Analysis [60 hours]

Statistical Analysis is a process of collecting and analysing data to uncover underlying patterns and trends to support organisations adopting data-driven strategies. Learners will gain the knowledge of describing real world data using statistics and charts. They will also learn how to perform estimation and inferences of population parameters for making decisions and prediction. Learners will also develop competencies in probability and statistics concepts required for statistical analysis using software tools and libraries which are essential to Data Science and Machine Learning.


Microcontroller Applications [90 hours]

This module provides learners with knowledge of microcontrollers and microcontroller programming. Topics covered include microcontroller architecture, C programming, and interfacing. This module will enable learners to develop microcontroller-based applications.


Electronic Communication Systems [60 hours]

This module covers the building blocks and the principles on how a typical electronic communication system operates. It provides the learners with knowledge of the nature of signals in both the time and frequency domain and explains how information signals may be transmitted using modulated carrier signals for analog and digital systems. The module also covers the types of transmission media and the fundamentals of antenna. At the end of the module, learners will be able to apply these concepts to analyse and test basic electronic communication systems.


Electronic System Design Project [90 hours]

This module provides learners with the ability to take a project from product specifications through circuit design, printed circuit board design, assembly and testing to a final product that is vital to electronic engineers in todayâ€™s world of electronic gadgets and devices. In this module, learners will be introduced to the operating principles of commonly used electronic circuits and their design considerations. Learners will also be equipped with essential printed circuit board (PCB) design knowledge through hands-on design work on computer-aided PCB design tools for multi-layer printed circuit boards and acquired skills in soldering, packaging and testing.


Communication and Personal Branding [30 hours]

In todayâ€™s competitive environment, a strong personal brand sets one apart from others. In this module, learners will develop techniques to impress and persuade others to accept their ideas. They will also learn how to communicate effectively over e-mail. To support their job search, learners will use digital portfolios to showcase their abilities, as well as gain the knowledge and skills to promote their capabilities in their cover letters and resumes. They will also acquire skills to project themselves positively and for performing well at interviews.

Application Development & Cloud Services [60 hours]

This module focuses on development of knowledge and skills to develop applications on smart devices that interface to cloud services for better user experience and data management. Topics covered include consuming Web APIs, JSON data exchange format, relational database and cloud service integration that form a crucial part in the architecture of a connected solution.


 


Database Design & Applications [60 hours]

This module focuses on using a relational database management system (DBMS) to design, implement and manage database applications. Topics covered include relational database schema design, data modelling, data manipulation and query formation using the Structured Query Language (SQL). Development of secure database applications will also be discussed.


Mobile Computing Project [60 hours]

This module aims to equip learners with the ability to design and develop efficient applications on mobile computing platforms that address business requirements. Emphasis will be placed on enabling learners to work on the different stages of the application development life-cycle from design, development, testing and deploying of a mobile application. Topics covered include mobile device capabilities, connectivity infrastructure, mobile-to-cloud interfacing and client-server applications.

Learners will further acquire software debugging techniques by identifying and rectifying software errors using the development tools. This module aims to equip learners with the knowledge and skills to evaluate, test and characterise wireless communication and network systems. Learners will be able to evaluate suitable wireless systems for deployment. Topics covered include system design considerations, radio propagation, wireless connectivity technologies and test and measurement techniques.


IoT System Project [60 hours]

This module integrates knowledge and skills in wireless and embedded technologies to implement Internet of Things (IoT) applications. The module also covers basics of project management, in particular, issues related to the organization, planning, realization and control of projects. Concepts will be reinforced through case studies.


Supply Chain Management [60 hours]

This module covers the design, control, operation and management of supply chain systems, including procurement, movement and storage of raw materials, work-in process inventory and finished goods from point-of-origin to point-of-consumption. The impact of supply chain management and digitalisation on the competitiveness of the organisation will also be discussed. With this knowledge and skills, learners will be able to plan and manage supply chain processes.

This module covers the fundamentals of data visualization, visual analytics and dashboard creation for business applications. Learners learn to work with business data such as customer purchase behaviour, sales trends or production bottlenecks to uncover trends and correlations for decision making. Microelectronics

Core Modules

  • Semiconductor Technology
  • Wafer Fabrication Processes
  • IC Design & Test Project
  • Communication & Workplace Success

Semiconductor Technology [60 hours]

Major topics covered in this module include classification of cleanrooms, a study of major IC devices, parametric testing, failure analysis and reliability engineering. Practical skills pertaining to these topics will be realized in laboratory to enhance the understanding of semiconductor technologies in a real manufacturing environment. At the end of the module, learners will be able to apply the knowledge gained to real life engineering problems involving parametric testing, failure analysis and reliability engineering.


Wafer Fabrication Processes [60 hours]

This module provides learners with essential knowledge of wafer fabrication processes for IC (integrated circuit) chips in the semiconductor manufacturing industry. Major topics covered include oxidation, diffusion, photolithography, etching, ion implantation, thin film deposition, chemical mechanical polishing, wafer cleaning, and process integration. Practical skills pertaining to these technologies will be emphasized in the laboratory to enhance the understanding of wafer fabrication in a semiconductor-manufacturing environment.


IC Design & Test Project [60 hours]

This module covers the technologies and design methodologies of integrated circuits using CMOS devices. Learners will be able to apply appropriate techniques and design methodologies in designing the mask layout of integrated circuits with CAD tools. It also introduces the test and measurement of semiconductor devices including digital devices, mixed-signal devices and memory devices.


Communication and Workplace Success [30 hours]

This module is designed to build an understanding of the design and development process of embedded systems. Learners will acquire software development techniques by integrating peripherals and interfaces in a microcontroller. Learners will further acquire software debugging techniques by identifying and rectifying software errors using the development tools. This module uses high performance ARM-based microcontroller as a learning platform.

  • Data Analytics & Machine Learning
  • Autonomous Mobile Robotics

Data Analytics & Machine Learning [60 hours]

Organisations harness the benefits of data analytics and machine learning to gain a sustainable competitive edge in the market. Through this module, learners will be able to apply data analytics to gain insights from data and pre-processing data techniques to develop effective machine learning applications. They will also be able to build analytics applications for scenarios in manufacturing and other relevant sectors by ap-plying machine learning algorithms.


 


Autonomous Mobile Robotics [60 hours]

Autonomous mobile robotics is an important solution for Industry 4.0 in optimising de-livery processes, increase productivity and flexibility. Learners will gain a broad over-view of key components used in autonomous mobile robot (AMR) and their integration approach for implementing a wide variety of industry applications. Learners will be able to explain the different types of sensors, actuators and connectivity used in an AMR deployment. They will be able to apply Simultaneous localisation and mapping (SLAM) for path planning and navigation, and use sensor fusion techniques for obstacles avoidance.


 


Automation Systems & Control [60 hours]

Automation systems are crucial to many industries that desire to increase productivity, improve product quality and streamline processes. Through this module, learners will be equipped with knowledge and skills about various fieldbus systems, application & interface of sensors and actuators, programming of automation controller and implementation of Internet of Things (IoT) in automation systems. They will also be able to develop, integrate and troubleshoot industrial automation systems.


 


IoT System Architecture & Technology [60 hours]

Internet of Things (IoT) play a prominent role in todayâ€™s digital economy, it is a growth engine that fuels business opportunities and product innovations. Through this module, learners will build knowledge of system architecture, enabling technologies as well as business and innovation opportunities of IoT. They will develop competencies in edge to cloud integration, communication protocols and device security of IoT systems. Learners will also be able to deploy real world solutions in many areas of applications in IoT.


 


Smart Healthcare Applications [60 hours]

This module aims to provide learners with knowledge and skills to develop smart healthcare applications. Topics covered include acquisition of vital signals, wearable sensors and devices, signal processing methods, e-health services and applications development. Learners will be able to apply and integrate technologies for smart healthcare applications in the healthcare industry.


Sustainable Building Management System [60 hours]

This module aims to equip learners with the knowledge and skills in modern building automation and sustainable technologies. Topics covered include building automation network protocols, building control software, sustainable energy management, smart sensors and Internet of Things (IoT) implementation. With this skills and knowledge, learners will be able to develop building automation solutions and collaborate on projects related to sustainable and green building.
