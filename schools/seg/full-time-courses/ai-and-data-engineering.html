About the Course

Unlock your full potential with AI & Data

Everything is connected to the internet – it could be the heart rate collected from our smart watch, or the efficiency of the engine that powers the plane we fly in. The devices we use daily generate data, which provides experts with insights to help save lives. For example, doctors can monitor a patient’s health to predict a heart attack, and engineers can predict performance issues in the engines. Have you ever wondered how all this data is processed and translated into insights?

Artificial Intelligence (AI) transforms how traditional engineering industries operate. Data is collected from these different sources, like Internet of Things (IoT) sensors, smart devices and on board machine systems. Then, AI solutions are built to process the data, remove repetitive manual work, automate processes, predict preventive actions and more.

Check out Straits Times' news article on our course Nanyang Polytechnic to co-develop AI and data engineering course with industry partners.

The Diploma in AI & Data Engineering is for you if you want to acquire skills in applying AI solutions across various engineering sectors.

Benefits

  • Learn all about how engineering, AI and data skills are applied across engineering verticals.
  • Acquire skills in data analytics and AI.
  • Develop highly sought-after skills and learn how AI and automation can help improve business operations.

 

NYP Professional Competency Model (NYP-PCM) - its unique features

The Diploma in AI & Data Engineering features the NYP-PCM – a new teaching model developed by NYP which lets students stay ahead and prepares them for the digital economy.

Industry Relevance

Co-developed by industry leaders like Microsoft, YITU, AISG, OMRON, Tiger Analytics, STMicroelectronics and Nvidia, it is informed by real industry intelligence and needs.

Professional Accreditation

You gain additional industry certifications from Microsoft, Nvidia and AI Professionals Association (AIP) while you complete your diploma, putting you ahead of the pack.

Agile Response

The curriculum reacts dynamically and quickly to industry changes. You'll be equipped with the latest skills & knowledge.

Integrated Learning

Concurrently learn multiple disciplines. Be taught by guest lecturers from Microsoft, AISG, OMRON, Tiger Analytics and STMicroelectronics.

In Year One, students in the Diploma in AI & Data Engineering will be studying the same modules with all the other engineering students as part of the Common Engineering Program (CEP). In total they will be studying 13 modules in Year One.
From Year Two onwards, the NYP Professional Competency Model for the Diploma in AI & Data Engineering consists of:

  • 22 Competency Units – Within each interdisciplinary Competency Unit, skills and knowledge from different disciplines are integrated and taught holistically.  This enables the students to fulfil specific work-tasks upon completion. Completing the Competency Units will also equip our students with nine Course Competencies.
  • Four Competency Canvases –  where skills and knowledge learnt are assessed for competency, together with a Work-Integration-Unit (WIU) which is a workplace contextualised project. You have to complete all four to graduate.
  • One Final Year Project (FYP).
  • One Internship Placement (ITP).
  • 10 General Studies Modules (GSMs) – these modules give you exposure to other areas of interest and allow you to go beyond your chosen area of specialisation.

Students enrolling in the Diploma in AI & Data Engineering under the Professional Competency Model will have to complete 22 Competency Units (CmUs). Within each interdisciplinary CmU, skills and knowledge from different disciplines are integrated and taught holistically, enabling students to fulfil specific work tasks upon completion.

Completing the CmUs will also equip our students with the nine Course Competencies (CCs) below.

  • Engineering Product Management
  • Production Management
  • Engineering Business & Operations
  • Robotic & Automation Technology Applications
  • IoT Management
  • Data Processing
  • Data Security & Governance
  • AI Modelling & Applications
  • AI System Design & Development

Students will also need to complete Work-Integration-Unit (WIU) which can be in the form of an internship or project that mirror work situations where students can demonstrate their skills and competencies.

Algebra [60 hours]

This module provides learners with the basic mathematical principles and tools necessary to build their engineering foundation. It will enable them to apply mathematical methods, tools and notations proficiently in the analysis and solution of engineering problems. Topics covered include mathematical functions (polynomials, exponential, logarithms, step functions and trigonometry), commonly used in engineering complex numbers, determinants, matrices and vectors.


 


Introduction to Engineering [60 hours]

This module introduces learners to different engineering disciplines by focusing on the application of relevant core engineering disciplines. Topics covered include roles and responsibilities of an engineer, ethics, and sustainability. Learners will engage in the practice of engineering through the process of conceiving, designing, and implementing solutions to engineering problems, individually and in teams. Through this, learners will also be able to hone their creative thinking and problem-solving skills, build synergistic teamwork and enhance their communication skills.


 


Electrical Principles [60 hours]

This module covers electrical fundamentals and their applications in electrical and electronic circuits. Topics covered include principles of electricity, power sources, resistive, capacitive and inductive circuits. Learners will need this knowledge to analyze, interpret and solve engineering problems.


 


Fundamentals of Mechanics [60 hours]

This module provides learners with the fundamentals of engineering mechanics. Topics covered include Newton laws, statics of rigid bodies, moment of inertia and bending moments. Fundamentals in dynamics that include kinematics and kinetics of particles will also covered in this module. Learners will acquire fundamental engineering competence to understand and perform engineering-related activities related to statics and dynamics.


 


Programming [60 hours]

The practice-oriented module introduces learners to software development methodology. The learners will learn about problem-analysis, problem-solving techniques and develop essential programming skills. The learners will be able to create flowcharts, develop algorithms, write structured programs, test and debug programs.


 


Effective Communication Skills [30 hours]

Communicating well is a vital life skill, benefitting all aspects of our lives from professional life to social gatherings. It is thus important to be proficient in both written and oral communication. This module will enable learners to communicate effectively in different settings and on different collaborative platforms. There will be opportunities to practise and deliver various types of presentations, to learn to competently handle questions, and to effectively incorporate verbal and non-verbal elements when speaking. Learners will also acquire media and information literacy and be able to produce clearly written and well-structured reports and proposals, following standard citation and referencing guidelines.


Calculus [60 hours]

This foundation module provides learners with the essential knowledge in calculus. This module helps learners in building skills for solving engineering problems encountered in their course of study. Topics include differentiation and integration of commonly used functions in engineering applications.


 


Engineering Exploration Project [30 hours]

This module aims to develop learners’ innovative and enterprising skills while developing creative and technically robust solutions to real-world challenges. Learners will be able to effectively relate design thinking in the process of exploring innovative and enterprising solutions addressing the real needs that will bring about positive social change.


 


Workplace Digital Skills [30 hours]

This module enables learners to use word processing software to create, modify and style document/report effectively and efficiently. Learners will use spreadsheets to manage, organise and model data as well as to perform analysis and report generation. Creation of customised, dynamic and multimedia-style presentations using software tools will also be covered. Through hands-on practices, learners will gain these skills and apply them in project work in preparation for the digital workplace.


 


Fundamentals of Innovation and Enterprise [30 hours]

Learners will develop attributes that are pervasive and synonymous with being innovative and enterprising for career and life. Through this module learners will develop positive practices when working with data, propose ideas using user-centric approaches and design processes, determine and locate resources, and leverage collaborative practices to formulate solutions.


 


Internet and Web Development [60 hours]

This module covers the design and development of web pages using HTML mark-up language and the CSS styling language to equip learners with essential knowledge and skills in developing effective front-end web applications. Learners will learn the techniques in creating responsive web design for multi-form factor devices. Learner will also gain an overview of internet infrastructure and its underlying technologies.


Materials Technology [60 hours]

In this module, learners will acquire knowledge of properties and applications of engineering materials, including ferrous and non-ferrous metals, polymers, composites, and ceramics. Learners are exposed to various material properties tests and characterization, material processing methods and different material failure modes. Learners will be able to select appropriate materials for specific engineering applications.


 


 


Engineering Drawing & Modelling [60 hours]

In this module, learners will gain knowledge and skills to model the engineering parts and interpret drawings that are derived from the 3D models. Topics covered include orthographic projection, sectional views, assembly drawing, geometric dimensioning and tolerancing. Learners will acquire the necessary skills in communicating ideas and concepts through engineering drawings, which is one of the necessary and critical skills needed in the design process.


 


 


General Studies

To provide you an all-rounded education, NYP offers General Studies Modules (GSMs). So besides taking modules related directly to your chosen diploma course, you will get exposed to areas beyond your usual field of study. Through a combination of prescribed and elective inter-disciplinary GSMs, you will gain interesting, practical knowledge that can be beneficial to you as you prepare for the world of work and life after graduation.

Sensor Data Collection & Analysis [45 Hours]

Sensor data collection and analysis is crucial for a successful implementation of Internet of Things (IoT) application. Through this CU, learners will develop competencies in developing an IoT application using an array of smart sensor that collects a variety of data types such as status, location, automation and actionable data obtainable in smart sensors. Learners will be competent in using smart sensors to transfer data through a gateway and the various network protocol stack such as transport, network and link layer. Learners will also be competent in performing basic analysis on data collected to solve engineering related problems.


 


Intelligent Control Systems [45 Hours]

Intelligent control is one of the keys enabling technologies for the increase in functionality and safety of many critical applications. Through this CU, learners will be competent in developing an intelligent control system using electrical control devices, sensors, I/O interfacing and programmable logic control (PLC). Learners will also be competent in developing and applying logic programming to control automation equipment.


 


Engineering Design [15 hours]

Engineering design is required to develop quality, cost-competitive products in the shortest time possible. It is the method that engineers use to identify and solve problems using sustainable design principles, taking into considerations the importance of design for production and environment. Through this CU, learners will develop the competencies in engineering design communication and to work with cross-disciplinary teams in recommending ideas and improvement. Learners will be competent to perform testing and evaluation of system or components that meet the requirements, the specifications, and the ease of production.


 


Data Preparation & Visualization [60 Hours]

Data preparation and visualization are essential for any intelligent data-driven applications. Through this CU, learners will develop competencies in performing basic data pre-processing such as data fusion, cleaning and augmentation on raw data. Learners will be competent in making sense of data through various data visualization techniques. Learners will also be competent in working collaboratively in teams to present data in an effective way to gain deeper insights into engineering data, as well as to present efficient information visualization to different levels of stake holders in an organization.


 


Data Security [15 Hours])

Data security enables the effective implementation of protective measures to prevent unauthorized access to data. Through this CU, learners will develop competencies in applying concepts and principles of data security from both personal and enterprise levels. Learners will be competent in identifying the latest data security trends and threats and describing the incidents and security policies that are happening around the world.


 


Data Management [30 Hours]

Data Management comprises all the techniques related to managing data as a valuable resource to enhance sensemaking in individuals and organisations. Through this CU, learners will develop competencies in using relational database management system (DBMS) and operational database model to store, retrieve and manipulate data. Learners will also be competent in applying non-SQL technologies such as key-value store, document, columnar and graph databases to store, retrieve and manipulate data storage.


 


Machine Learning [60 hours]

Machine learning allows the user to feed a computer algorithm an immense amount of data and have the computer analyze and make data-driven recommendations and decisions based on only the input data. Through this CU, learners will develop competencies in applying machine learning algorithms, including both supervised and unsupervised learning for AI modelling, using industry standard libraries/framework. Learners will also be competent in determining appropriate machine learning algorithms for different use cases across engineering verticals.


 


Cloud Computing & Platforms [30 hours]

Cloud computing offers businesses with scalable computing resources hence saving them on the cost of acquiring and maintaining them. Cloud computing is the on-demand availability of computer system resources, especially data storage (cloud storage) and computing power, without direct active management by the user. Through this CU, learners will develop competencies in identifying basic strategies for transitioning to the cloud through applying the breadth of services available in the cloud including compute, network, storage, and security. Learners will build competencies in digital fluency on cloud computing platforms by identifying which technology is available and what tools is best to achieve the desired outcomes.


 


Data Engineering Project (Work Integration Unit, WIU) [30 hours]

With data engineering, trends in data sets can be found and algorithms can be developed to solve problems and help make raw data more useful. Through this WIU, learners will be competent in applying engineering project management and communicating effectively to implement sensor data collection, storage, retrieval, ingestion, processing, and monitoring to satisfy engineering application needs. Learners will also be competent in using the data collected to provide insights and make sense out of this data to solve engineering related problems.


 


Engineering Product Lifecycle Management [15 hours]

Engineering Product Lifecycle Management (PLM) is critical to increase efficiency and quality, reducing costs and errors occurring during the engineering process. It is the process of managing the entire lifecycle of a product from inception, through engineering design and manufacture, to service and disposal of manufactured products. Effective PLM helps organizations in coping with the increasing complexity and engineering challenges of developing new products for the global competitive.


 


Internet of Things Application [60 Hours]

The power of Internet of Things (IoT) lies in its ability to change the way businesses think and operate. Through this CU, learners will develop competencies in architecting, developing and deploying IoT applications in the engineering field to solve problems. Learners will also be competent in applying technologies such as edge computing and data analytics in IoT systems to monitor, automate and provide insights for decision making.


 


Production Technologies [15 hours]

Production Technologies are key factors for the success of engineering enterprise. The aim is to create products that fulfil customer needs with a minimum required resource. Through this CU, learners will develop competency in thinking creatively to evaluate, decide and articulate different production technologies that allow for maximum product quality and reduced production costs.


 


Operation Management [30 hours]

Operation management is the running of the day-to-day operations to ensure the highest level of efficiency and productivity within an engineering firm. Through this CU, learners will develop competencies in communicating with various stakeholders on the management of resources, the production of products and finally, the distribution of finished products and services to customers. Learners will be competent in applying operations strategy, designing the product and process, planning, and scheduling resources and managing the inventory from the supply chain that are required by day-to-day engineering business and operations. Learners will also be competent in using AI tools to help stakeholders to make vital decisions in an organisation.


 


Applied Deep Learning [60 hours]

Deep Learning improves machine learning accuracies when trained with huge amount of data. Deep learning is a class of machine learning algorithms that uses multiple layers to progressively extract higher-level features from the raw input. Through this CU, learners will develop competencies in developing AI modelling and application using state of art deep learning algorithms. Learners will be competent in evaluating various architectures of deep learning algorithms and identifying the appropriate architecture to make sense and gain insights about the datasets. Learners will also be competent in adapting these algorithms and conduct hyperparameters tuning to solve engineering problems.


Computer Vision [60 hours]

Deep Learning improves machine learning accuracies when trained with huge amount of data. Deep learning is a class of machine learning algorithms that uses multiple layers to progressively extract higher-level features from the raw input. Through this CU, learners will develop competencies in developing AI modelling and application using state of art deep learning algorithms. Learners will be competent in evaluating various architectures of deep learning algorithms and identifying the appropriate architecture to make sense and gain insights about the datasets. Learners will also be competent in adapting these algorithms and conduct hyperparameters tuning to solve engineering problems.


 


Natural Language Processing [60 hours]

Natural Language Processing (NLP) plays a more prominent role in bridging computer and human communication. Through this CU, learners will develop competencies in applying NLP algorithms to enhance efficiencies and solve engineering challenges. Learners will be competent in adapting NLP applications such as text classification and conversational AI to perform prediction, gain insight and sense making out of text data.


 


AI Engineering Project (WIU) [30 hours]

With AI Engineering, AI models are generated to replicate a decision process to enable automation and understanding. Through this WIU, learners will develop competencies in identifying, using, and conducting tuning on various AI models in Natural Language Processing and/or Computer Vision to improve the efficiency and effectiveness within different engineering verticals to solve engineering problems. Learners will be competent in building AI models to obtain insights and make sense out of it for effective product life cycle management.

Production System Management [45 hours]

Production System Management is to ensure the business produces quality products that can satisfy the needs of customers on a regular basis. Production system management refers to planning, organising, directing, and controlling of production activities. Through this CU, learners will develop competencies in how different systems such as enterprise resource planning, production planning, shop floor monitoring and control can be integrated together for production system management. Learners will be able to identify and understand the entire production value chain from order entry to product delivery in both at the shop floor and enterprise levels. Learners will be competent in using various software tools and techniques for the monitoring and management of the production system.


 


Automation & Robotics Technology [45 Hours]

Automation and robotics are key enabling technologies to increase engineering efficiency. Through this CU, learners will develop competencies in programming robots to complete tasks. Learners will be competent in applying advanced technologies to automate engineering process. Learners will also be competent in choosing the appropriate sensors and actuators, as well as developing programs for automation controller in automation systems.


 


Supply Chain Management [30 hours]

Supply chain management has become an integral part of business. Supply chain management has the power to boost customer service, reduce operating costs and improve the financial standing of a company. Supply Chain Management (SCM) is about the management of the flow of goods and services. Through this CU, learners will develop competencies in applying digital tools in SCM to help organisation to achieve its goals with a global mind set. Learners will also be competent in applying the various global supply chain strategies for the integration of supply chain components into a coordinated system to enhance service level and reduce system-wide cost.


 


AI-Enabled Business Models [15 hours]

Effective Business Models are critical for a successful business leveraging on advanced technologies such as AI. Through this CU, learners will be competent in identifying the strategies and technologies required to support the organization in developing an innovative AI-based culture. Learners will also be competent in using AI to create a business model that is inclusive.


 


Big Data Processing Platforms & Applications [60 Hours]

Big data helps organizations harness their data and use it to identify new opportunities. Big data is to analyse, extract information from or deal with data sets that are too large or complex to be dealt with by traditional data-processing. Through this CU, Learners will develop competencies in evaluating and identifying the right big data processing platforms. Learners will be competent in using Big Data processing platforms to perform sense making through acquiring, storing, cleaning, querying and performing analytics to gain insights on various engineering related big data datasets.


 


AI Privacy, Security & Ethics [15 Hours]

AI will be the forefront in this digital economy. However, privacy security and ethics are the few key concerns that must be dealt with through policy and careful solution design. Through this CU, learners will develop competencies in discussing the implications of AI driven decision on biases and ethics. Learners will also be competent in the relevant privacy acts, regulation, laws and policies. Lastly, learners will also be competent in describing how AI driven decision making process influences stakeholders and the best practices to promote inclusivity in using AI.


 


AI Application Development [60 hours]

AI application development is critical for deployable and scalable AI solutions. AI application development is a complex process and requires not only AI skills, but also modern agile software development skills. Through this CU, learners will be competent in developing customer-oriented AI applications in production environment using techniques including DevOps and MLOps. Learners will also be competent in working collaboratively in teams to develop AI applications.


 


AI Solution Architect Project (WIU) [30 hours]

AI solutions are created to solve immensely difficult issues in various industries. Through this WIU, learners will develop competencies in architecting the tailored AI solutions and identifying required tools/products. Learners will be competent in working collaboratively in teams to develop an AI solution proposal by identifying engineering problems in the selected theme. Learners will also be competent in developing a unique selling proposition to explain how the proposal can solve the problem and to provide insights, sense making for decision making process.


 


AI Solution Development Project (WIU) [30 hours]

Scalable and deployable AI applications are critical for successful AI adoption across industries. Through this WIU, learners will develop competencies in working in teams collaboratively to develop a AI solution through using appropriate data processing techniques, deploying suitable AI models to solve business problems, to gain insights and making sense out of the data for better decision making in the selected theme. Learners will also be competent in deploying solution using containerization technologies to enhance the scalability. 


General Studies

To provide you an all-rounded education, NYP offers General Studies Modules (GSMs). So besides taking modules related directly to your chosen diploma course, you will get exposed to areas beyond your usual field of study. Through a combination of prescribed and elective inter-disciplinary GSMs, you will gain interesting, practical knowledge that can be beneficial to you as you prepare for the world of work and life after graduation.

Final-year students will also can undertake a full-time project and participate in a local or overseas internship programme for one full semester.


 


Four Competency Canvases – where skills and knowledge learnt are assessed for competency, together with a Work-Integration-Unit (WIU) which is a workplace contextualised project. You are required to complete all four Competency Canvases to graduate.

The following list reflects the Competency Units and Work-Integration Units mapped under each Competency Canvas.

  • Sensor Data Collection & Analysis
  • Data Preparation and Visualization
  • Data Security

  • AI Privacy, Security & Ethics
