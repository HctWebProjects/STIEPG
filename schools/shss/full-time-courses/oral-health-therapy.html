Oral Health Therapy (C72)

Range of Net ELR2B2: 5 to 11

About the Course

Offered with support from the Health Promotion Board, the National University Centre for Oral Health, the Singapore Dental Association and government polyclinics, this course teaches core skills in operative and preventive dentistry, dental hygiene, oral-health promotion and clinic management. Learn to perform procedures like polishing, scaling, root planing, fillings and extraction of milk teeth. Plus, gain hands-on experience in specialised simulation laboratories and during clinical attachments.

What You'll Study

Core Modules

  • Head and Neck Anatomy
  • Dental Anatomy and Histology
  • Biological Science 1

Head and Neck Anatomy [30 hours]

This module examines the anatomical structures and functions of the orofacial region relevant to oral health practice. The module will focus on the hard and soft tissues of the head and neck region. At the end of the module, the students will be able to identify and differentiate the anatomical structures, functions and landmarks of the orofacial region.


Dental Anatomy and Histology [60 hours]

This module provides the student with the basic foundations of Dental Anatomy which deals with the macroscopic and microscopic study of the structure and development of teeth and their supporting structures. Students will be able to differentiate the forms and functions of primary and secondary dentition and understand the pathological changes that could occur in the oral cavity.


Biological Science 1 [45 hours]

This module examines basic principles of human biology and studies the musculoskeletal, haematological, cardiovascular and respiratory systems. The structure, functions and abnormities of cells and tissues will be discussed. At the end of the module, the student will be able to describe the various cells, tissues and systems of the human body.


Inorganic and Physical Chemistry [60 hours]

This module will provide students with fundamental knowledge of inorganic and physical chemistry that are important for the understanding of Oral Health Sciences. At the end of the module, students will be able to understand the concepts of bonding, structure, nomenclature and applications of chemical reactions.


Oral Health Promotion and Practice 1 [30 hours]

The module introduces students to the roles, responsibilities, legislation of Oral Health Therapists in Singapore and the fundamentals of health promotion. The concepts of general health, prevention of disease and health promotion will be covered. At the end of the module, students will understand the employment, career and educational opportunities of an Oral Health Therapist.


Dental Therapy Practice 1 (120 hrs)

The module will introduce basic principles of microbiology, good ergonomic practice, philosophy and concepts of restoring teeth. The process, classification of tooth decay and the usage of appropriate materials and dental instruments will be covered. At the end of the module, students will be equipped with knowledge on disease transmission which will enable them to apply it in the practice of infection control. Students will also be able to develop fundamental operative skills necessary for dental therapy practice.


Dental Hygiene Practice 1 (120 hrs)

The module will introduce basic principles of microbiology, good ergonomic practice, periodontology, basic pre-clinical skills on instrumentation and use of oral hygiene aids to promote self-care. Students will be taught basic structure and function of the periodontium and the pathogenesis and host response of periodontal disease. Predisposing and host factors that modify the disease process are highlighted to serve as foundation for the management of periodontal diseases. At the end of the module, students will be equipped with knowledge on disease transmission which they will apply in the practice of infection control.


Effective Communication Skills [30 hours]

This module provides an overview of the interpersonal communication strategies and skills needed for effective oral communication in the work place. The module will also enable learners to produce clearly written reports and proposals with good structure. At the end of the module, students will be able to understand ethics in writing by recognising and avoiding plagiarism and following standard citation and referencing guidelines.


Oral Pathology [45 hours]

This module introduces students to the general principles of disease initiation, progression, transmission and body responses. It also examines diseases affecting the oral and dental tissues. Students will learn to recognize the manifestations of diseases and conditions in the head and neck region.


Dental Chemistry and Technology (60 hrs)

This module will cover the fundamental physical, chemical properties and use of dental materials. This module also aims to provide students with the knowledge and advancements on different IT and digital systems in dentistry. The module will also equip students with skills on clinical photography as part of a comprehensive oral assessment.


Biological Science 2 (45 hrs)

This module examines the function and dysfunction of the nervous, gastrointestinal, endocrine, urinary and reproductive systems and the general effects of aging. At the end of the module, students will be able to describe the structures, functions of the different systems of the human body.


Organic and Biochemistry (45 hrs)

This module will provide students with fundamental knowledge of topics in organic chemistry that are important to life sciences. These include concepts of bonding, structure, isomerism, nomenclature and reaction mechanisms of the different functional groups of organic compounds. This module will also provide students with basic knowledge about the chemistry of biological processes. Students will be taught the importance of enzymes, metabolism and processing of biological information.


Oral Health Promotion and Practice 2 (30 hrs)

The focus of the module will be the role of diet and nutrition in general and oral health, intrapersonal models of health promotion and behaviour change. The module will also examine the role of fluoride in caries prevention at an individual level. At the end of the module, students will be able to relate epidemiology to general and oral health and be able to evaluate an individualâ€™s health status, social and psychological well-being throughout the lifespan.


Dental Therapy Practice 2 (120 hrs)

This module builds on the foundations developed on infection control and good ergonomics. The focus will be on caries detection, risk assessments and management. The module will cover techniques in treating caries and defects on smooth surfaces of teeth. Students will also learn the use of adhesive restorative materials.


Dental Hygiene Practice 2 (120 hrs)

This module builds on the foundation developed on infection control and good ergonomics. The focus will be on classification of periodontal diseases and the principles of non-surgical periodontal therapy. The module will also cover basic pre-clinical skills on instrumentation and use of oral hygiene aids to promote self-care. At the end of the module, the students will be able to classify periodontal disease and discuss the principles of non-surgical periodontal therapy.


Fundamentals of Innovation and Enterprise (30 hrs)

Learners will develop attributes that are pervasive and synonymous with being innovative and enterprising for career and life. Through this module learners will develop positive practices when working with data, propose ideas using user-centric approaches and design processes, determine and locate resources, and leverage collaborative practices to formulate solutions.

Year 2

Core Modules

  • Oral Medicine
  • Dental Radiology Techniques & Anatomy
  • Pharmacology & Pain Management
  • Cell & Molecular Biology
  • Oral Health Promotion & Practice 3
  • Dental Therapy Practice 3
  • Dental Hygiene Practice 3
  • Research Methods and Statistics
  • Geriatric & Special Needs Dentistry
  • Oral Health Promotion & Practice 4
  • Dental Therapy Practice 4
  • Dental Hygiene Practice 4
  • Literature Appraisal & Academic Writing

Oral Medicine [30 hours]

This module will provide the students with knowledge on oral manifestations of oral and systemic diseases and medication. The module will equip students with skills on effective history taking and orofacial examination to aid in diagnosis and treatment planning. The module will also cover the correlation of treatment planning and underlying medical conditions. Students will learn the approaches in performing a dental triage and dental emergencies.


Dental Radiology Techniques & Anatomy [60 hours]

This module introduces the general principles and techniques of dental radiography. Students will learn the safety aspects, handling, exposure and processing of intra-oral and extra-oral radiographs. This module also aims to equip students with the skills necessary in indicating and interpreting periapical, bitewing, lateral cephalogram and orthopantomogram radiographs in child and adult patients.


Pharmacology & Pain Management [60 hours] ]

This module introduces the student to the basic principles of pharmacology, routes of drug administration, nature of drug action and pharmaco-kinetics. It includes the understanding of the management of adverse drug reactions and drug interactions. The module will cover drugs use in dentistry including antibiotics, analgesics, vasoconstrictors, sedatives, disinfectants and antiseptics, the administration of local anaesthesia and management of any complications arising from it. Students will learn the importance of recognizing and managing pain and anxiety in dental patients, especially in patients with pre-existing medical conditions or complications, using therapeutic and/or alternative methods.


Cell & Molecular Biology [45 hours]

This module will introduce students to the cell as basic unit of life in all living organisms and appreciate the biological activities that occur within the cell and molecular level. Students will learn the machinery and processes that are fundamental to biology at the level of molecules, and the physical considerations for these interactions.


Oral Health Promotion & Practice 3 [30 hours]

The module will equip students on sociological concepts, analysis of the broad socio- cultural interactions, interpersonal models of health promotion and behaviour change, and communication skills needed to manage complex situations in the dental environment. The module also introduces students to the basic principles of law and ethics as applied to healthcare practice. Related professional issues in dental care delivery, documentation and defensive dentistry will be discussed.


Dental Therapy Practice 3 [120 hours]

The module will cover infection control, good ergonomics, behaviour management in children and principles of cavity preparation in restoring primary dentition. Students will be equipped with holistic treatment planning skills that will be developed during interactions with the supervising dentists and impression taking. The students will be introduced to techniques involved in extractions of deciduous teeth under local anaesthesia.


Dental Hygiene Practice 3 [120 hours]

The module will cover infection control, good ergonomics, classification of periodontal diseases and importance of post debridement evaluation and maintenance. The students will demonstrate knowledge learned on instrumentation, infection and ergonomics on student patients. The module will also equip students with skills to function at entry level in clinical environment.


Research Methods and Statistics [30 hours]

This module introduces students to the basic concepts and principles of research methodology and statistical analysis. Students will be introduced to areas of research designing related to conceptualisation, operationalisation, validation, sampling, examines various methodologies for data collection, various aspects of probability, descriptive and inferential data analysis.


Geriatric & Special Needs Dentistry [30 hours]

The aim of this module is to develop the knowledge and skills of students in the recognition and management of patients with special needs. This includes geriatric and special needs patients who are affected physically, medically, psychologically and socially. Students will understand their role within a multi-disciplinary team for holistic management of such patients.


Oral Health Promotion & Practice 4 [30 hours]

This module will introduce the students to the various systemic, behavioural and psychological disorders. The module will also focus on the importance of evidence- based practice, community and institutional models of health promotion and behaviour change. The students will also learn community project management skills.


Dental Therapy Practice 4 [200 hours]

This module provides opportunities for the students to integrate theory and practice in Dental Therapy in clinical practice settings and offers them opportunity to provide oral health care within their scope. The students will learn to work independently and interact professionally as an essential member of the dental team in the planning, management and delivery of simple preventive and restorative treatments. The students will demonstrate competencies and skills in strict infection control procedures in patient care, management of cooperative patients, clinical and radiographic assessment of caries, extraction of mobile deciduous teeth under local anaesthesia and discussion and presentations of clinical case studies.


Dental Hygiene Practice 4 [200 hours]

This module provides opportunities for the students to integrate theory and practice in Dental Hygiene in clinical practice settings and offers them opportunity to provide oral health care within their scope. The students will learn to work independently and interact professionally as an essential member of the dental team in the planning, management and delivery of oral health care in gingivitis patients. The students will demonstrate competencies and skills in strict infection control procedures in patient care, screening individual patients, calculus classifications, managing patients with gingivitis discussion and presentation of clinical case studies on gingivitis.


Literature Appraisal & Academic Writing [30 hours]

The aim of this module is to equip students with literature appraisal skills. The students will learn to identify types of literature reviews, conduct critical appraisal of research- based articles for a chosen topic. Students will also demonstrate correct in-text and end-text referencing and understand the implications of plagiarisms.


Evidence Based Practice [30 hours]

This module provides students with the opportunity to systematically identify a dental or related issue for further investigation and come up with a proposal such that the result would lead to direct or indirect benefit to the students themselves in the educational process or in the delivery of health care. It will equip learners with the ability to seek and critically appraise studies and to apply evidence into clinical practice.

Year 3

Core Modules

  • Oral Health Promotion & Practice 5
  • Dental Therapy Practice 5
  • Dental Hygiene Practice 5
  • Innovation & Enterprise Project Proposal
  • Future Ready Professionals
  • Oral Health Promotion & Practice 6
  • Dental Therapy Practice 6
  • Dental Hygiene Practice 6
  • Innovation & Enterprise Project

Oral Health Promotion & Practice 5 [30 hours]

This module will introduce the students to basic public health policies and social challenges. The students will learn the use of fluoride at a community level, to plan and implement a community project and conflict management skills.


Dental Therapy Practice 5 [200 hours]

This module provides further opportunities for the students to integrate theory and practice in Dental Therapy in clinical practice settings and offers them opportunity to provide oral health care within their scope. The students will learn to work independently and interact professionally as an essential member of the dental team in the planning, management and delivery of complex preventive and restorative treatments. The students will demonstrate competencies and skills in strict infection control procedures in patient care, management of un-cooperative patients with assistance from supervisor, extraction of carious deciduous teeth or root stumps under local anaesthesia and discussion and presentation of clinical case studies.


Dental Hygiene Practice 5 [200 hours]

This module provides further opportunities for the students to integrate theory and practice in Dental Hygiene in clinical practice settings and offers them opportunity to provide oral health care within their scope. The students will learn to work independently and interact professionally as an essential member of the dental team in the planning, management and delivery of oral health care in mild periodontitis patients. The students will demonstrate competencies and skills in strict infection control procedures in patient care, screening individual patients, calculus classifications, managing patients with mild periodontitis. The module will also equip students with the skills to discuss and present clinical case studies on mild periodontitis.


Innovation & Enterprise Project Proposal [60 hours]

The module builds on Evidence Based Practice module where students will further develop their projects. Students will learn about IRB approval, seeking consent and approval from relevant parties involved, systematic implantation of planned project in accordance to the principles of research methods, collection and storage of data in adherence to personal data protection act.


Future Ready Professionals [30 hours]

This module will explore and identify educational and professional options after graduation. The module will also cover workplace diversity and career resilience. The students will be exposed to the legislation and registration of the Oral Health Therapy profession in preparation to seek employment after graduation.


Oral Health Promotion & Practice 6 [30 hours]

This module introduces students to registration and legal administrative paper work essential for registration with the Singapore Dental Council and the set up and relevance of forensic dentistry in relation to emergencies and disasters. The students will learn to identify and manage abuse and neglect, the healthcare framework in Singapore that consists of health care systems, segregations, nursing homes, dental facilities and alternative models to deliver dental care. Students will attempt to plan and implement a community project.


Dental Therapy Practice 6 [200 hours]

This module provides further opportunities for the students to integrate theory and practice in Dental Therapy in clinical practice settings and offers them opportunity to provide oral health care within their scope. The students will learn to work independently and interact professionally as an essential member of the dental team in the planning, management and delivery of oral health care in pre-schoolers and children with special needs. The students will demonstrate competencies in strict infection control procedures in patient care, management of un-cooperative patients with minimal assistance from supervisor. The module will also focus on the roles of the oral health therapists in special areas of dentistry and inter-professional education. The module will also equip students with the skills to discuss and present clinical case studies, function in various clinical environments.


Dental Hygiene Practice 6 [200 hours]

This module provides further opportunities for the students to integrate theory and practice in Dental Hygiene in clinical practice settings and offers them opportunity to provide oral health care within their scope. The students will learn to work independently and interact professionally as an essential member of the dental team in the planning, management and delivery of oral health care in moderate periodontitis patients. The module will also focus on the roles of the oral health therapists in special areas of dentistry and inter-professional education. The students will demonstrate competencies in strict infection control procedures in patient care and management of patients with moderate periodontitis. The module will also equip students with the skills to discuss and present clinical case studies on moderate periodontitis and function in various clinical environments.


Innovation & Enterprise Project [60 hours]

The module builds on the Project Proposal; the students will further develop their projects. The focus will be on data collection and analysis of data using statistical principles, presentation of project findings and results in a journal style written report and designing a scientific poster. Students may be required to present their findings to the dental industry, public and explore submission to refereed journals or develop prototype for industry use.

  • Career Prospects


    As an oral health therapist, you can practise in areas such as:

    • school dental service at the Health Promotion Board, in school dental clinics or at mobile dental clinics
    • private dental practices or dental institutions
    • oral health promotion
    • clinical audit
    • other dental-related industries (marketing, sales & training)

    Our graduates are always in demand. Look forward to an exciting career as an oral health therapist in Singapore and aboard, and expect an attractive and competitive pay with opportunities for further career progression.
